# Lore

This is Alore. At its heart lies the realm of Azergad, where eight nations have lived under an ancient Pact for three Generations of a thousand years each. The immortal Paragons of Man have guided their peoples since the Pact was signed at Thilidan.

## Inazria

Inhabited by hardy farmers and nomadic cattle herders, Inazria is a coastal country just east of Kystriland separated by the Violet Sea. The capital of Inazria is at Thilidan, the largest city in the known world built upon the Thilidan River, in the central part of the country. Thilidan's sprawling metropolis is thought to be the largest and most important in the world. The city invites opportunities of every kind from every race of man. Yet, the recent population expansion has created an imbalance of uneasiness in the population. A city of extremes, Thilidan harbors the world's wealthiest families living mere kilometers from the largest slums in the world.

The northern part of the country is spacious, fertile and tropical. The region is split between tropical forests to the west and dry, temperate plains to the east by the Marram mountain range. The crossroads city of Marlis is a traveler's preferred path through the rugged Marram to Bisengal in the northeast. Inazria's hub of ideation and creation, Marlis was founded next to huge natural deposits of materials like iron, copper, coal, and rare gryxite ore. Along the gulf to the northeast, Windfall is home to traders and pioneers amidst a largely unexplored landscape. The coast's arable wetlands provide a wonderful blueprint for irrigated farmlands. Home to many humans, halflings, satyrs and orcs, Windfall boasts a reputable hub for land and naval trade with Inazria's neighbors Bisengal and Kystriland. The thick, flat jungle landscape across the inland is known for numerous small lakes and rivers as well as deep Guphatis shrouded in mystery. Rural villagers tell stories of unthinkably vast sprawling dungeons and tunnels, filled with unthinkably hostile adversaries.

To the east of Thilidan lies Azelliot, the breadbasket of Azergad. Most of the land is a flat plain supporting temperate forests full of life. North Azelliot becomes a warm and humid marshland as it approaches the Marram Mountains towards the Bisengal border. As Azelliot stretches eastward, the landscape splits up into cold islands before reaching the tundra lands at the "bottom of the world".

Thilidan and central Avarazhan are split by the Veldt, an impressively dense forest of extremely tall trees famously home to the Veldtwin elves. The western forest abruptly halts at the Bashan coast before picking up once again across the chaotic Asuran wildland islands. To the south of the Veldt lies the coastal plateau region of Avarazhan.

West of Inazria lies the expansive Kystrilands, the largest island chain in the world. According to the Guphati and Kaikone authorities, there are more than 20,000 islands spanning the longitude of several nations with varying degrees of civilization. Different islands are home to many different races and people groups. Northern islands tend to be sandy, rocky and volcanic, while Southern islands tend to be tropical and heavily forested. The largest islands in the chain reside in the geographical center, which tend to be urbanised and are farthest away from mainland Inazria. The islands generally form an "arc", which faces the mainland. Islands on the outer ring of the arc tend to be extremely dangerous due to the presence of unpredictable natural activity and vastly untamed and large wildlife.

## The Paragons of Man

In the earliest days of Azergad, the ancient races were left to bring order to the world. Each race produced a sole individual to guide them through the new frontier. The immortal Paragons of Man are the purest of heart, the wisest in mind, and the highest in spirit.

The Paragons soon arranged a constitution that formed the great nations. The pact formalized country barriers, created international roads for trade and travel, identified a common language separate from national languages, and established a loose guide for maintaining peace at large. These nations included the great plains of Azeliott (Satyrs), the great Marram mountains (Hyzalians), the dry tundra of Bisengal (Orcs), the deep dark woodlands of Avarazhan (Elves), the vast chain of islands called Kystriland (Dwarves), the lowland marsh called Zilwynbar (Halflings), the vast Violet Sea (Kelanari), and the tropical peninsula of Inazria (Humans), where the pact was signed in Thilidan. That day signified the first day of the first month of the first Generation of time, where the earliest known records began.

Hours later, the peace was in shambles. The Hyzalian named Zhu'kal, the White-Cold Wizard, had discovered an arcane power unlike anything ever seen before. His newfound "magic" caused a violent snowstorm to erupt over tropical Thilidan. Zhu'kal's intent was clear from the beginning: eliminate the Paragons at any cost. The Paragons hid as the wind and hail stayed strong for 3 dusks, while Zhu'kal and his zealous minions hunted them. On the third day, Sir Zaomyth the Gracious alone confronted Zhu'kal, and tried to reason with him to halt the storm; Zoamyth's pleas, utterly ignored, were met with fierce combat. Zaomyth fought back, and after slaying the last of Zhu'kal's followers, the White-Cold Wizard cast a spell to banish Zaomyth to an unknown plane. The brutal storm had taken many lives across Azergad.

Finally, on the dawn of January 4th, 1 1G, a bright light washed across the landscape. In a flicker, the storm subsided. Zhu'kal and his followers were nowhere to be seen or heard from again.

Members of the Paragons were said to be the most noble and wise of their representative races, providing a combination of impressive characteristics and high regards in the bloodline. They seem to be immortal, guiding their followers still three generations later. The Paragons are undoubtedly the greatest leaders Azergad has ever seen.

Each Paragon embodies a distinct virtue, keeps their seat in their people's homeland, and leads an order of Paladins sworn to bear that virtue into the wider world. A Paladin pledges to their order by belief, not by birth.

- **Narthex, Father of Peace** — The Paragon of the Humans resides at the Hall of First Light in Thilidan. He embodies patience and the conviction that violence must end where it began. He founded the Concord, a multinational Paladin order whose white-mantled knights act as neutral mediators on the treaty-roads of Azergad. Concordant Paladins swear to offer parley before steel. Chronicles credit Narthex with seventy-three major mediations since the Pact, including the Treaty of Marram. Narthex has refused every crown ever offered him.
- **Karhu, Queen of Justice** — The Paragon of the Orcs presides over the High Tribunal at the citadel of Khurz-Marn, in the dry tundra of Bisengal. She holds the highest bench in Azergad. Her fervor for justice is famous, as is the swiftness of her verdicts (and her less-than-typical mercifulness). The Tempered, her Paladins, ride at her writ as judge, jury, and executioner against evils of many kinds. They hunt bandit-kings, slave-traders, oathbreakers, and rogue magisters who outrun their own nation's law. Karhu led the Bisengalian campaign that ended the Hyzal's place upon the Marram.
- **Valadorf, Keeper of the Law** — The Paragon of the Dwarves rules from the High Court of Kalindor in central Kystriland. He embodies precedent, order, and the sanctity of the sworn word. He maintains the Great Ledger, in which every signed accord of the three Generations is bound and recopied by hand, including the trade compacts and commercial codes that bind the eight nations. His Paladins, the Pillars of Kalindor, answer to two sworn duties. The first is the physical defense of the Ledger and the Paragons themselves. The second is the enforcement of commercial law across Azergad. Throughout Kystriland ports and counting-houses, a Pillar's drawn shield is the final word on smuggling, fraud, and broken trade compacts.
- **Ashiera, Seer of Will** — The Paragon of the Elves dwells in Quietsong, beneath the deepest groves of Avarazhan. She embodies foresight, patience, and the long answer over the loud one. She rarely sits at the Council of Seven and is almost never seen by common mortals. A single glance from her is said to read the true willpower of any soul she meets, including what they intend, what they fear, and what they have hidden from themselves. Her Wardens of Will are an order in her own image, cloistered for decades of training and sworn to silence in public. They are loosed only against threats whose roots cross generations, such as hidden cults, oaths sworn in shadow, slow corruptions of court and crown, and any seed of Te that quickens unseen within a bloodline. Their long lifespans and experience of past Generations also make them sought-after counsel.
- **Calrien, High Shepherd of Alore** — The Paragon of the Kelanari holds no fixed court, traveling across the wild reaches of Azergad as her duties demand. She and her Shepherds regard the world itself as their ward. Her Shepherds answer to two sworn callings. The first is to rescue those in peril far from civilization, whether they are lost sailors, stranded caravans, or pilgrims swallowed by the jungle. The second is to defend the wild places of Alore from those who would waste or despoil them.
- **Myrdin the Meek** — The Paragon of the Satyrs walks the open plains of Azelliot. He takes no permanent seat and refuses every title beyond his name, though nobody knows why he chooses to live as he does. He embodies humility and the conviction that the smallest soul is worth the same as the highest. He speaks against evil when he sees it, and he reviles the undead. His Oracles travel across Azergad, healing and teaching the sick and poor. They wear no heraldry, accept no payment, and never draw their blades first.
- **Ostro, Author of Knowledge** — The Paragon of the Halflings resides at the Great Library at Zilwynbar. He embodies wisdom, memory, and counsel. Over the Generations he has gathered the world's knowledge, building his Library up as the foremost authority on every craft of Azergad, magical and otherwise. It also holds a copy of every chronicle of consequence, the most important of which are read aloud each year at the annual New Solar Reading. The Arbiters, his Paladins, are renowned for their intellect and serve as advisors to kings, magistrates, and adventuring companies. Senior Arbiters are also versed in certain rites and minor workings drawn from the Library's deeper shelves. They are sworn never to withhold a true word in counsel, and never to speak a false one.

## Beyond the Paragons

After over 900 years of development, numerous small wars and conflicts, and integral ideas and inventions, Karhu sought to punish the Hyzalians for the acts of their ancestors. The Hyzalians were, and are even still, poorly understood. Seemingly lacking in technical and social progression, the Paragon-less Hyzalians clung to traditions and mystical, primal practices. As a culture, they do not trust outsiders and refuse outsider counsel.

Emboldened Orcish forces greatly outnumbered and outpaced the pathetic and disjointed Hyzalian military response, driving those few remaining Hyzal to the peaks of the highest Marram summits. Now greatly reduced in population and space, the Paragons met to herald peace once more. The Treaty of Marram, signed September 11th, 890 1G, redrew the country boundaries to expand Bisengal into parts of the Marram. In exchange for great military aid, and as a plea for help from the few remaining Hyzal, Marram was dissolved and the land was divided into Bisengalian and Inazrian jurisdictions. The Hyzal remained in cloistered tribal communities atop the harsh mountain landscape, although officially gaining lawful Inazrian citizenship, though Hyzalians would still live in self-imposed isolation even thousands of years later. This great event marked what would become the turn of the Generation, an event that now occurs once every 1000 years.
