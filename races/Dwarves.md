## Dwarves

Descendants of men from the volcanic tropics of Kystriland, the dwarves are hardy, creative, and strong-willed. Grand kingdoms carved in molten mountains and complex underground communities dot the beaches found throughout the island chain. The dwarves are courageous and wise, known to stand their ground against overwhelming opposition; they make a point to keep their word and take great pride in their ancient heritage.

A dwarf is steady by nature. Because dwarves live long lives, they are often slow in relationships with other men which they expect to outlive. Natural skeptics, they may be quick to judge others lacking strong morality or clear foundation. A wise dwarf knows that their greatest allies are ones with the temperament to hold to their virtues no matter the cost.

The tallest of the Kystriland men stand just above 4 feet tall, but are sturdy enough in stance to weigh between 200 to 300 pounds or more. Dwarves have thick hair all over their bodies, with the burliest men growing long, extravagant beards in brown, black, red, and blonde shades. Dwarves are young until they reach the age of 50, then typically live for 300-400 years, maintaining strong memories their whole lives. The predominant dwarven people groups, the Caekone (see-kone) and the Guphati, have buried feud after feud for thousands of years. The two nations shift between times of peace and times of bloody rivalry. Dwarves of all kinds are welcome in most of modern-day Kystriland, though many rural villages cling to their traditions.

Kystriland's mountains stretch high yet deep underground and are filled with precious materials such as gold, pik (also known as white gold), and betharyl (a high-quality material used for ornate equipment). Most of the world's trade involves materials and logistics that flow through this region. Dwarves are generally fascinated by the endless possibilities of metals and jewelry attainable through the forge. The desire of wealth leads most dwarves to a life of seafaring, immersing themselves across a variety of cultures and climates.

### Traits

- **Ability Score Increase**: +2 Constitution
- **Age**: Dwarves mature at a similar rate to humans, but they're considered young until they reach the age of 50. On average, they live about 350 years.
- **Size**: Dwarves stand between 4 and 5 feet tall. Your size is Medium.
- **Speed**: Your base walking speed is 25 feet. Your speed is not reduced by wearing heavy armor.
- **Darkvision**: Accustomed to life underground, you have superior vision in dark and dim conditions. You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.
- **Dwarven Resilience**: You have advantage on saving throws against poison, and you have resistance against poison damage.
- **Dwarven Training**: You have proficiency with the battleaxe, handaxe, light hammer, and warhammer. Additionally, you gain proficiency with the artisan's tools of your choice: smith's tools, brewer's supplies, or mason's tools.
- **Stonecunning**: Whenever you make an Intelligence (History) check related to the origin of stonework, you are considered proficient in the History skill and add double your proficiency bonus to the check, instead of your normal proficiency bonus.
- **Languages**: You can speak, read, and write Common and Kystri. Kystri is a mixture of various dwarven languages with added Common words, and is primarily a trade language taught to educated Dwarves. Kystri is full of hard consonants and sounds, which often spill over into whatever other language the dwarf might speak.

### Guphati

The Guphati dwarves inhabit the expansive mountain chains of the greater Kystrilands. Guphati dwarves are known to be full of grandeur and ambition with their pursuits. Guphati architects find satisfaction from constantly out-doing themselves, crafting and erecting increasingly elaborate structures and systems. The Caekone consider their behavior predictable and dull, remarking that their mountain cousins are often too obtuse to laugh at a simple joke. Living amongst sheer cliffs and smoldering heat, Guphati dwarves are particularly athletic and are strong climbers. Guphati dwarves have light tan to gray skin, brown, red, or black hair, and black, red, or golden eyes.

- **Ability Score Increase**: +1 Strength
- **Language**: You can speak Guphati, a distinct language with many long words and phrases. Though common among the Guphati, it is rarely spoken by foreigners or in foreign lands.
- **Summit Lore**: Always add your proficiency bonus when you make a Nature check recalling information related to underground or mountainous terrain.
- **Athletic**: You are proficient in Climbing, and do not suffer disadvantage on Athletic checks made on mountainous terrain.

### Caekone

As a Caekone dwarf, you'll be quick-witted, brave, and naturally charming. The Caekone dwarves are a creative people known for their shipwrights and survival instincts. Many are as well-read as they are lighthearted, enjoying deep philosophical talks in-between their bustling lifestyles. These dwarves of the coast are just as excellent traders and leaders as they are swimmers. Due to this behavior, Guphati dwarves stereotypically have difficulty taking the Caekone seriously. Caekone dwarves have darker tan to red skin tones. Their hair tends to be lighter colors, including blondes, light browns, white, and light red. Their eyes can be bright, tropical shades of blue or green.

- **Ability Score Increase**: +1 Charisma
- **Language**: You can speak Caekone. The Caekone language is known to be loud and light in tone. It is the origin of many widely-used trading terms in both standard Kystri and Common.
- **Life on Water**: Caekone dwarves never have a disadvantage when in combat on the water. Additionally, Caekone dwarves are proficient in Swimming and are capable of treading water for great lengths of time even while wearing light armor.
- **Silver Tongue**: You are proficient in Persuasion, especially when in negotiations with one or more individuals.

### Solkota

Hidden deep within the tropical rainforests of the outer Kystriland islands, the Solkota dwarves have adapted to life in the jungle canopy and forest floor. These dwarves are known to hunt often and are surprisingly agile among Kystri-kind. The Solkata are famous for their ancient temples and ornate tools of magical quality, commonly featuring the betharyl that exists in nearby offshore deposits. They are master botanists and alchemists, using the jungle's abundant plant life to create potent medicines and poisons.

- **Ability Score Increase**: +1 Wisdom
- **Language**: You can speak Solkota, a unique language with an ancient script. It is quite an isolated language and notoriously difficult to learn.
- **Jungle Adaptation**: You have resistance to poison damage and advantage on saving throws against diseases.
- **Botanical Knowledge**: You have proficiency with herbalism kits and the Medicine skill. You can identify nearly any plant of tropical origin and know its general properties.
- **Canopy Dweller**: You have a climbing speed of 25 feet when climbing trees.

### Palcha

Hidden within the secluded Palcha Valley on Bisengal's western frontier live the secluded Palcha people. Once rulers of a mighty empire that stretched across the western reaches of Bisengal and into the foothills of the Marram Mountains, the Palcha wielded their innate telekinetic magic to dominate neighboring peoples for nearly four centuries. Today, they continue to live in small, fortified communities carved into the valley's steep walls, maintaining a warrior culture built around discipline, mental focus, and the mastery of their hereditary force magic. Outside contact is rare, as many Palcha continue to view the outside world with pity and immense distrust. However, the Palcha diaspora exists in various pockets, particularly around the region once ruled by their ancestors.

- **Ability Score Increase**: +1 Intelligence
- **Language**: You can speak Palcha, a dialect so closely related to Caekone that speakers of one can understand the other with minimal difficulty. However, Palcha incorporates many archaic terms and formal constructions that mark the speaker as distinctly foreign to most Caekone ears.
- **Telekinetic Touch**: You know the mage hand cantrip. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for this spell.
- **Disciplined Mind**: You have proficiency in one mental skill of your choice: Arcana, History, Investigation, or Insight.
