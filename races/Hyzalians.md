## Hyzalians

Little is known from the world outside about the peculiar Hyzalian. Careful and wise, Hyzalians prefer to live in small, cloistered populations dotted across the summits of the unforgiving Marram. They certainly value tradition and deep understanding, reciting the wisdom of the past before deciding on the future.

Ancient Hyzalian literature is of a distinctly religious nature. According to Inazrian mythology, they are said to be the original descendants of Tamid'Zelnah, the being which first unified men in mind and spirit. However, the Hyzalians were cursed: a perfect creation cast down to mortality and left to think for themselves. Some Hyzalians adhere to this ancient religion even in contemporary times, though their numbers are few. 

Hyzalians are known to view outsiders with distrust. To them, outsider civilization has perverted and twisted their concepts of morality to be deeply unrecognizable. The few Hyzal that venture away from their homes often return with stories of misguided, impulsive, self-absorbed and untrustworthy men; on the flip side, the other races often consider Hyzalian culture to be primitive, rigid and conservative. Though earning their trust is a trial of its own, a Hyzalian companion is a lifelong ally.

Hyzalians are elegant creatures. Men are around the same height as humans, while women tend to be a few inches shorter. Hyzalian skin is very thick and lustrous like metal. Skin tones are various shades of gray: light and dark, and sometimes with brown or blueish tints depending on the family. Their maw is large, holding a grand set of teeth including two exaggerated upper canines. Their ears protrude from the skull significantly and are quite circular in shape. Their eyes are shades of brown, yellow, red, or violet. Healthy males are heavy and weigh between 250 to 400 pounds on average, while women are typically 50 to 60 pounds less. Hyzalians reach adulthood at age 25 and can live for many centuries.

Hyzalians are famously known for their thick, bright white hair that grows like a lion’s mane around their heads. While soft, it is exceptionally durable and difficult to cut. As per tradition, Hyzalians do not ever cut their hair. It grows until the Hyzalian reaches adulthood and, once cut, can never grow back. A Hyzalian with very long hair is held in very high esteem, while a shaved Hyzalian is shameful and not to be trusted.

Te-Hyzalians trace their ancestry to Te, who is said to be the first Hyzalian to discover darkness. Extremely little is known about them apart from stories and folklore. A living Te-Hyzalian encounter has not been documented for many centuries, though rumors and tales of their presence lingers on.

### Traits

- **Ability Score Increase**: +2 Wisdom, +1 Constitution: Hyzalians naturally desire understanding and truth, while their tough skin gives them an advantage in harsh environments. Their rigid skin and awkward frames restrict their movement on the ground.
- **Darkvision**: You can see in dim light within 60 feet as bright light, and darkness as dim light in shades of gray.
- **Size**: Hyzalians are tall and imposing. Your size is Medium.
- **Speed**: Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
- **Marram Fortitude**: Hyzalians are gifted climbers and feature impressive athletic bodies. You have proficiency in Athletics and advantage on checks to climb rocky surfaces.
- **Thick Skin**: Hyzalian skin is difficult to penetrate and can be resistant to heavy blows. When you take slashing or piercing damage, you can use your reaction to reduce the damage by your proficiency bonus. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.
- **Glare Resistance**: Hyzalians have adapted to a snowy life above the clouds. You have advantage on saving throws against being blinded.
- **Languages**: You can speak, read and write Hyzalian. Hyzalians that regularly interact with outsiders and/or have an above average Intelligence score (11) may also speak, read and write Common. The Hyzalian language combines physical gestures with a variety of sounds, but is highly expressive and rich in vocabulary. None but the most studious of outsiders can speak or understand Hyzalian.
