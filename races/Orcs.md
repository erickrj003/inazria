## Orcs

The Orcs of Bisengal are a diverse and hardy people shaped by the unforgiving landscapes they call home. Orcish civilization values strength, endurance, and unwavering loyalty to clan and country. Orc society emphasizes personal honor achieved through overcoming adversity, whether in combat, survival, or mastery of one's craft.

Orcs are powerfully built, standing around 7 feet tall with broad shoulders and muscular frames that speak to generations of physical adaptation. Their rough skin ranges from deep forest green to weathered grayish-brown. Their prominent lower canines and strong jaws reflect their carnivorous heritage, while their keen senses serve them well in Bisengal's harsh environments. Most orcs live 60-80 years, reaching maturity by age 16.

Throughout the last thousand years, several Orcish empires have risen and fallen across Azergad. In antiquity, it was the Dramakha empire that first rose to prominence, asserting long-lasting dominance over the other races; the conquests of empires since have all sought to regain their past glory. 

At one time, a revived Dramakha empire conquered vast swaths of human and elven territory through superior Orcish metallurgy and warfare tactics, only to fragment when the ruling clan was challenged by dozens of rival houses simultaneously. Another time, a reunified offensive built the greatest naval force the world had ever seen and controlled trade routes across the Violet Sea, but collapsed when a succession crisis turned into the War of Burning Ships. Just a hundred years ago, a seemingly stabilized version of the Empire controlled large portions of Azelliot, Zilwynbar and northern Inazria. An empowered public protested, leading to deadly resistance efforts on many borders. As Dramakha power was weakening, the squabbles of the ruling class quickly erupted into ethnic tensions between Gushbeks, Barithanis and Yaz’togalis. A new conflict exploded into the devastating three-way civil war that has left the Orcish people broken, impoverished, and confined to the hostile badlands of Bisengal today.

### Traits

- **Ability Score Increase**: +2 Strength
- **Age**: Orcs mature by age 16 and live up to 80 years.
- **Size**: Orcs are larger and bulkier than humans. Your size is Medium.
- **Speed**: Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
- **Darkvision**: You can see in dim light within 60 feet as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light in shades of gray.
- **Relentless Endurance**: When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead. You can't use this feature again until you finish a long rest.
- **Savage Attacks**: When you score a critical hit with a melee weapon attack, you can roll one of the weapon's damage dice one additional time and add it to the extra damage.
- **Powerful Build**: You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.
- **Languages**: You can speak, read, and write Common and Orcish. Orcish is a harsh, guttural language with sharp consonants that carries well across Bisengal's windswept landscapes.

### Gushbek

The Gushbek inhabit the scorching deserts of southern Bisengal, where they have mastered survival in one of the world's most inhospitable environments. These orcs are leaner than their cousins, with sun-darkened skin and the ability to go for days without water. They are renowned as fearless raiders and master beast-tamers.

- **Ability Score Increase**: +1 Dexterity
- **Language**: You can speak Gushbek, a guttural language commonly used throughout rural Bisengal that is mutually unintelligible with standard Orcish.
- **Desert Adaptation**: You require half as much water as normal and can navigate deserts without getting lost. You do not consider deep sand to be difficult terrain.
- **Beast Taming**: You have proficiency in Animal Handling. As an action, you can communicate simple concepts with desert creatures within 30 feet for 10 minutes. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

### Barithani

In the dense undergrowth of the Veldt, the Barithani have adapted to life in perpetual twilight beneath the canopy. These orcs are expert hunters and trappers, moving silently through the forest floor while tracking prey that would elude other races. Their settlements are built into root systems and fallen logs, nearly invisible from above.

- **Ability Score Increase**: +1 Wisdom
- **Language**: You can speak Barithani, a more elegant Orcish language that borrows words and phrases from Veldtwin.
- **Forest Camouflage**: You have proficiency in Stealth. When in forest terrain, you can attempt to hide even when only lightly obscured by foliage.
- **Tracker's Sense**: You have proficiency in Survival. You can track creatures that passed through an area up to 7 days ago, and you can identify the creature's approximate size and direction of travel.

### Yaz'togal

The Yaz'togal claim the highest peaks of Bisengal's mountains, where they have built fortress-cities that guard the passes between nations. These orcs are the largest and strongest of their kind, adapted to thin air and brutal cold. They serve as Bisengal's elite warriors and are responsible for maintaining the ancient watchtowers that monitor Hyzalian movements.

- **Ability Score Increase**: +1 Constitution
- **Language**: You can speak Yaz'togal, a loud and harsh-sounding dialect of Orcish. It is only usually used among the upper and royal class of Yaz'togal society - speaking it proficiently is a sign of status.
- **Mountain Born**: You're acclimated to high altitudes and cold climates. You're naturally adapted to cold climates, as described in chapter 5 of the Dungeon Master's Guide. Additionally, you gain resistance from cold-based damage.
- **Sure-Footed**: You have advantage on ability checks and saving throws against being knocked prone.
