## Satyrs

The Satyr is an abundantly common race of humanoids recognized by prevalent bovine-like legs heads adorned with grizzly hair and horns who hail from the temperate hilly forests of Azelliot. Satyrs are generally modest creatures, with a natural tendency towards diplomacy rather than violence, but are quite the energetic folk and notoriously unable to sit still. Communities of satyrs are often very large, as they crave the company of many individuals. Many Satyr tribes are animal herders and active nomads who do not settle in one place for very long; instead, they move their homes regularly once the resources of the area no longer suit the needs of their livestock. Satyrs are well-known for their exceptional hospitality, welcoming any outsider who loves music and is an easy laugh.

Satyrs are quite tall, with adults ranging from 6'1" up to 7'6" and taller. Most satyrs are also quite thin, only weighing up to 160 to 240 pounds despite their height. Satyrs often have dark brown, black, or white hair from head to toe, and plenty of thick hair on their chest and face. Satyrs typically have hoofed feet, large hairy legs, and various styles of bovine horns which differ by tribe; Their ears are pointed, longer than an elf's, and protrude outward from the skull. Male horns are much more pronounced than the female's. Like a coat-of-arms, no two families of Satyr have the same set of horns. Satyr skin ranges from dark brown to a mild tan, and eyes tend to be shades of yellow, brown, black, or green. Despite this, satyrs typically have larger features than humans, including large jaws, hands, heads and facial features. Satyrs living in large, clustered communities often take up roles as educators or politicians. In Inazria itself, satyrs are typically known to be charismatic yet influential leaders.

### Traits

- **Ability Score Increase**: +2 Charisma
- **Age**: The oldest Satyrs can live to be up to 80 or 90 years old and reach adulthood at about 18.
- **Size**: Satyrs are slender yet quite tall compared to humans. Your size is Medium.
- **Fast Speed**: You have a high base walking speed of 35 feet.
- **Low-Light Vision**: Satyrs have sharp vision and great depth perception. In low-light conditions, you can see within 60 feet in shades of gray.
- **Tough Horns**: Although Satyrs are light creatures, their heads and horns are incredibly hard and make for potent weapons. The Satyr can make melee unarmed strikes dealing 1d4 + Strength modifier of bludgeoning damage
- **Swift Movements**: Satyrs are proficient in Acrobatics due to their naturally deft physique.
- **Languages**: You can speak, read, and write Common and Satyr. Satyr is elegant and full of soft syllables, but is only properly spoken at loud volumes. The language of each Satyr people group is mutually intelligible with standard Satyr.

### Jaziko

The most common variety of satyr, the Jaziko inhabit the temperate forests and plains that make up the bulk of Azelliot. The Jaziko thrive in their native lands as aggressive hunters while maintaining a cultural sensitivity to Mother Nature's needs. Naturally outgoing and friendly, the Grassland satyrs are generally welcoming towards outsiders of all kinds.

- **Ability Score Increase**: +1 Dexterity
- **Language**: You speak Jaziko. Jaziko is known to be beautiful and energetic, with a wider vocabulary than standard Satyr.
- **Persuasive**: At the beginning of the game, you choose to gain proficiency in either Intimidation and Persuasion, reflecting a natural ability to be agreeable yet intimidating due to their horns and size.
- **Social Skills**: You are naturally talented and able to speak or play something well without much preparation. You gain proficiency in Performance and a single musical instrument of your choice.

### Lendolo

The Lendolo inhabit the underexplored wetlands where Azelliot meets the northern marshes. They live simpler, laid-back lives in stilted villages that rise and fall with seasonal floods. These pale satyrs have adapted to the aquatic environment, developing webbed hooves and water-resistant fur. They are known to be deep thinkers, contemplative artists and philosophers, creating beautiful sculptures from driftwood and chanting slow, haunting music that echoes across the marshes. A village typically consists of many fishermen and artisans.

- **Ability Score Increase**: +1 Wisdom
- **Language**: You can speak Lendolo, known for its poetic structure and philosophical terminology. Lendolo requires high concentration to speak, and conversations often take time due to the language's slow pace.
- **Marsh Walker**: You can walk on water up to 1 foot deep without sinking, and you have a swimming speed of 20 feet.
- **Artistic Soul**: You have proficiency in one type of artisan's tools and one type of musical instrument.

### Borshai

The Borshai roam the foothills between Azelliot's plains and the Marram mountains, following ancient migration routes with their herds of mountain goats and hardy cattle. These satyrs are shorter and stockier than their cousins, with thick woolly coats and curved horns adapted for headbutting. Extremely proud of their culture, they are renowned storytellers and musicians. They are the keepers and preservers of oral histories that span centuries. The Borshai are famous for having incredible memories.

- **Ability Score Increase**: +1 Constitution
- **Language**: You can speak Borshai, a more tonal dialect of Satyr. Speaking Borshai with confidence sounds like a chorale.
- **Mountain Adaptation**: You're acclimated to high altitudes and have advantage on Constitution saving throws against exhaustion caused by long hikes or extreme weather.
- **Oral Tradition**: You have proficiency in History. When recalling information about events that occurred within the last 200 years, you can add your proficiency bonus to the check.
