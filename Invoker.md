## Invoker

Arcane power runs in your veins, a living inheritance from an elder beast whose will still echoes through the world. Invokers do not study the arcane—they awaken it. Your spells, summons, and manifestations reflect the temperament and dominion of your progenitor. Two Invokers rarely mingle; rival bloodlines clash like storms, and even allies keep wary distance.

As with all classes in Inazria, your context supplies most of your identity and mechanics; the base class provides the core engine.

### Hit Points
- **Hit Dice**: 1d8 per invoker level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level**: 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels**: 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per invoker level after 1st

### Proficiencies
- **Armor**: Light armor
- **Weapons**: Simple weapons and shields
- **Tools**: One of your choice (artisan’s tools or musical instrument)
- **Saving Throws**: Charisma, Constitution
- **Skills**: Choose two from Arcana, Insight, Nature, Perception, Persuasion, Religion

### Equipment
You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:
- (a) a quarterstaff or (b) a simple weapon
- (a) a component pouch or (b) an arcane focus
- (a) explorer’s pack or (b) scholar’s pack

### Class Rules
- **Somatic Weaponry**. You can perform the somatic components of invoker spells even when you are wielding weapons or a shield. A weapon you are proficient with can serve as your spellcasting focus.
- **Blood Focus**. Your beast blood acts as your spellcasting focus.
- **Seamless Weave**. On your turn, if you both cast a spell and make a weapon attack, you can take them in any order and split your movement before, between, and after them.

### Spellcasting
You are an arcane spellcaster whose magic springs from your beast lineage.
- **Spell List**: You learn and prepare spells from the Invoker list. Your context may add additional spells or replace choices.
- **Cantrips**: You know two cantrips from the Invoker spell list at 1st level. You learn additional cantrips at higher levels as shown on the Invoker table.
- **Preparing and Casting Spells**: You prepare a number of Invoker spells equal to your Charisma modifier + your invoker level (minimum of one). You can cast any invoker spell you have prepared, using spell slots as shown on the Invoker table. You can change your prepared spells when you finish a long rest.
- **Spellcasting Ability**: Charisma is your spellcasting ability for your invoker spells. Your magic is powered by force of will and ancestral authority.
  - **Spell save DC** = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier
  - **Spell attack modifier** = your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier
- **Ritual Casting**: You can cast invoker spells as rituals if they have the ritual tag.
- **Spellcasting Focus**: Your blood focus substitutes for a spellcasting focus.

### Core Class Features
- **Ancestral Resonance (1st Level)**. Your blood hums with ancestral power. As you rely on your bestial ancestor for power, the strength of your own powers grow as well.
  - You have a **Resonance** pool that holds up to 3 points. You gain 1 point whenever you cast an invoker spell of 1st level or higher, or when you take the Help action targeting an ally and end your turn within 30 feet of that ally. Your Resonance resets to 0 when you finish a long rest.
  - You can gain at most 1 Resonance point per turn. You can gain Resonance from the Help action only while you are in initiative, and no more than a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.
  - When you cast an invoker spell, you can spend 1 Resonance point to apply one of the following effects to that spell (choose when you cast):
    - Empower: Add +1 to the spell’s save DC or +2 to one spell attack roll.
    - Displace: Move yourself or one target of the spell up to 10 feet; this movement doesn’t provoke opportunity attacks.
    - Aegis: One target of the spell gains temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.
    - Hamper: If the spell deals damage to or imposes a condition on a creature, that creature’s speed is reduced by 10 feet until the start of your next turn.
  - Your context grants additional ways to gain Resonance and unique spends (see Contexts).
- **Battle Weave (1st Level)**. After you cast a cantrip on your turn, you can move up to 10 feet without provoking opportunity attacks. This movement can occur before or after the cantrip resolves.
- **Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th Levels)**. Increase one ability score by 2 or two ability scores by 1 (to a maximum of 20).
- **Arcane Bulwark (7th Level)**. While you are concentrating on a spell, you gain a +1 bonus to AC and advantage on Constitution saving throws you make to maintain concentration.
- **Beast Echo (3rd Level)**. A permanent spectral echo of your progenitor attends you. It appears as a **Large** creature of your context’s aspect and acts on your initiative, immediately after your turn. It understands your languages and obeys your verbal or mental commands (no action required). The Echo lasts until it is reduced to 0 hit points, whereupon it reforms after a short or long rest. The Echo can serve as **your controlled mount** (using the standard rules for mounted combat); Medium or smaller creatures can ride it. If you ride the Echo, it acts only as your controlled mount and does not take a separate turn after yours. While you are mounted on the Echo, it can’t use Deliver Spell on that turn unless you spend 1 Resonance point when you cast the spell. If you don’t command the Echo on your turn, it takes the Dodge action.
  - You can command the Echo to take the Help, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Hide, or Deliver Spell actions. It can deliver a touch spell you cast as if it had cast it; you must be within 30 feet of the Echo when you finish casting, and the Echo must be within reach of the target.
  - As a bonus action, you can command the Echo to move up to its speed and take the Help action targeting a creature within 5 feet of it.
  - When you cast an invoker spell, you can spend 1 Resonance to let the Echo immediately take one of its actions (no bonus action required).
  - The Echo uses the stat block below and gains additional traits from your context.
- **Echo Ascendant (11th Level)**. Your Echo strengthens: its hit points increase, it gains a context movement mode, and it learns one special Reaction. It also gains resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage from nonmagical attacks. In addition, once on each of your turns when you cast a spell, you can have the Echo emit a minor surge that affects a creature within 5 feet of it with an effect based on your context (detailed in each context). This surge can’t damage directly and can’t stack with itself on the same creature.
- **Overcast (15th Level)**. When you cast an invoker spell of 1st level or higher, you gain temporary hit points equal to the spell’s level + your Charisma modifier. Until the end of your next turn, you also gain one of the following (your choice when you cast): +1 to your next spell save DC this turn, or +2 to your next spell attack roll this turn. You can’t benefit from Overcast more than once per round.
- **Apex of the Lineage (20th Level)**. Your maximum Resonance increases to 5. At the start of each of your turns, if you have 0 Resonance, you gain 1. When you cast an invoker spell, you can apply two different Resonance spends to that spell. Your Echo can occupy other creatures’ spaces and can’t provoke opportunity attacks.

#### Beast Echo (3rd‑Level Feature)
- **Large construct (spectral, mount)**, your alignment
- **Armor Class**: 12 + your proficiency bonus
- **Hit Points**: 4 × your invoker level + your Charisma modifier × 2
- **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Saving Throws**: uses your proficiency bonus; it has advantage on Dexterity saving throws against effects it can see
- **Skills**: Perception + your proficiency bonus, Stealth + your proficiency bonus
- **Damage Resistances**: force
- **Condition Immunities**: charmed, frightened, poisoned
- **Senses**: darkvision 60 ft., blindsense 10 ft., passive Perception 10 + PB
- **Languages**: understands your languages but can’t speak
- **Proficiency Bonus (PB)**: equals yours
- **Mount**: The Echo can be a controlled mount for you (PHB mounted combat). It moves on your turn and can Dash, Disengage, or Dodge when you command it (no action). Opportunity attacks provoked by your movement while mounted target the Echo.

- **Actions**
  - Help. The Echo aids a creature within 5 feet of it on the next ability check or attack roll it makes before the start of your next turn.
  - Deliver Spell. The Echo delivers a touch spell you cast as if it had made the touch. Use your attack and save DC as normal.
  - Swift Step. The Echo moves up to its speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

- **Reactions**
  - Interpose. When a creature within 5 feet of the Echo is hit by an attack, the Echo imposes a −2 penalty on the attack roll. You can use this reaction a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per short or long rest.

## Contexts

### Rekupuledo — Tides That Remember
A colossal dragon‑turtle is said to sleep beneath the world’s edge. Those chosen often first hear the world’s waters speak—rivers, rain, or tide—whispering lineage and duty. Rekupuledo invokers are supportive controllers who reduce battlefield danger, restrict movement, and fortify allies. His sign is the rainbow: peace after the storm.

- **Proficiencies** You gain proficiency with the trident, maul and hand crossbow.

- **Hallmark Spells** Your bloodline adds these to your known options as you gain levels (they count as Invoker spells for you):
  - 1st: create or destroy water, thunderwave, shape water (bonus cantrip; does not count against your cantrips known)
  - 2nd: warding bond, calm emotions
  - 3rd: tidal wave, water breathing
  - 4th: control water, guardian of nature (sea aspect)
  - 5th: maelstrom, wall of light (rainbow arc)
- **1st Level — Resonant Tides**. When you cast an invoker spell, you can spend Resonance to apply one of the following instead of (or in addition to) a base spend:
  - Undertow (1): Choose one creature affected by the spell within 60 feet. If it is Medium or larger, it must succeed on a Strength saving throw against your spell save DC or be pulled or pushed up to 10 feet. If it is Huge or larger, it has advantage on the save and is moved up to 5 feet on a failure. Creatures that are airborne and not over water are unaffected. A creature can’t be affected by your Undertow more than once per turn.
  - Tidewall (1): One target of the spell gains temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier and resistance to lightning, thunder, or cold (your choice) until the start of your next turn.
  - Slick Current (1): Create a 10‑foot line or a 10‑foot square of shallow water originating at a target of the spell. The area is difficult terrain for enemies until the start of your next turn. The first time each round an enemy enters the area or starts its turn there, it must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw (DC 10 or your spell save DC if you spend 2 Resonance) or fall prone.
  - Rainbow Aegis (2): Create a 10‑foot‑radius aura centered on a point you can see within 30 feet that lasts until the start of your next turn. Allies in the aura gain +1 AC and advantage on saving throws against being frightened.
  When you spend Resonance on any option above, you can move up to one 5‑foot cube of water you can see within 30 feet up to 15 feet as a bonus action (this uses the effects of shape water) to support your control play. Water moved this way persists for 1 minute.
- **2nd Level — Violet Shield**. If you cast a spell on your turn, you can use your reaction before the start of your next turn to reduce damage dealt to an ally within 30 feet by your proficiency bonus. You can instead spend 1 Resonance when you use this reaction to reduce the damage by your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier and move that ally up to 10 feet without provoking opportunity attacks.
- **3rd Level — Echo of Rekupuledo**. Your Echo gains a swim speed equal to its speed and can share your breathing. While adjacent to a creature, your Echo can grant it advantage on checks to escape a grapple. When you spend Resonance on Undertow, the Echo can move 10 feet as part of the effect.
- **6th Level — Rain Ward**. You learn warding bond. When you cast it, both linked creatures gain resistance to lightning and thunder while in rain or standing water, and each gains +1 AC in such conditions.
  Additionally, as a bonus action, your Echo can flood a 10‑foot square of ground you can see within 30 feet with shallow water for 1 minute. You can do this a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest, or by spending 1 Resonance when you cast an invoker spell. Water created this way counts as standing water for your class features.
- **10th Level — Tidekeeper**. When a hostile creature would enter or leave a contiguous area of standing water at least 1 inch deep that you can see within 30 feet, you can spend 1 Resonance to reduce its speed to 0 until the start of its next turn (once per creature per round). You must be able to see the movement.
  In addition, while your Echo is swimming, it can ferry one willing Medium or smaller creature that it is adjacent to at the start of its movement. That creature occupies the Echo’s space and moves with it until the end of the Echo’s movement or until it uses its reaction to disembark into an adjacent space. A ferried creature shares the Echo’s breathing while ferried.
- **14th Level — Ocean’s Patience**. Difficult terrain made of water doesn’t affect you or allies within 10 feet. When a creature fails a saving throw against one of your invoker spells, you can spend 2 Resonance to restrain it by undertow until the end of your next turn (escape DC = your spell save DC as an action). A restrained creature can’t benefit from effects that increase its speed.
- **18th Level — World‑Back Shelter**. You and your Echo are immune to lightning and thunder damage. When you cast a spell of 1st level or higher that deals lightning, thunder, or cold damage, choose up to your Charisma modifier creatures within 30 feet; each gains 10 temporary hit points.

### Duendekoko — Voice That Shapes Summer
A majestic mammal whose song moves mountains, sets the seasons, and blesses harvests. The calling comes while alone in nature, when the Invoker hears a sudden gust carrying Duendekoko's song. Duendekoko invokers are versatile sword‑and‑spell casters who shape earth, wind, fire, and sound while safeguarding companions and the natural order.

- **Proficiencies** You gain proficiency with the warhammer, greatsword and heavy crossbow.

- **Hallmark Spells** Your bloodline adds these to your known options as you gain levels (they count as Invoker spells for you):
  - 1st: earth tremor, healing word
  - 2nd: shatter, enhance ability
  - 3rd: erupting earth, plant growth
  - 4th: stone shape, aura of life
  - 5th: wall of stone, dawn
- **1st Level — Canticle of Summer**. When you cast an invoker spell, you can spend Resonance to apply one of the following effects instead of (or in addition to) a base spend:
  - Sunbrand (1): Once before the end of your turn, when you hit with a weapon attack, that attack deals an extra 1d8 fire or radiant damage (your choice), and you can push the target up to 5 feet. This extra damage increases to 2d8 at 11th level and 3d8 at 17th level.
  - Summer Guard (1): Until the start of your next turn, you gain +1 AC and temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.
  - Bounty (1): One ally targeted by or in the area of your spell gains +10 feet movement and ignores difficult terrain until the start of your next turn.
  - Quake Step (1): Raise or lower stone/soil in a 5‑foot cube within 60 feet to create low cover or remove it until the start of your next turn.
- **2nd Level — Bulwark Training**. You gain proficiency with medium armor. While you are wearing medium armor and not incapacitated, you have advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws made to resist being shoved or knocked prone. In addition, when you succeed on a Constitution saving throw to maintain concentration, you can move up to 5 feet without provoking opportunity attacks.
- **3rd Level — Echo of Duendekoko**. Your Echo carries your battle hymn.
  - Harmonic Aegis. When a creature within 5 feet of the Echo is targeted by an attack, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on the attack roll. You can use this reaction a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest, or by spending 1 Resonance when you cast an invoker spell.
  - Battle Hymn. As a bonus action, the Echo can empower an ally within 10 feet of it. The next weapon attack or spell attack that ally makes before the start of your next turn gains a +1 bonus to the attack roll and deals an extra 1d4 radiant damage on a hit.
- **5th Level — Martial Master**. You gain Extra Attack. On a turn in which you cast a cantrip, you can make one weapon attack as a bonus action.
- **6th Level — Duendekoko’s Mercy**. When you or an ally within 30 feet would fail a saving throw against being paralyzed, restrained, or petrified, you can spend 1 Resonance as a reaction to grant a reroll with advantage. If the reroll succeeds, the creature gains temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.
- **10th Level — Stone Singer**. When you cast a spell, you can spend 1 Resonance to shape a 10‑foot‑by‑10‑foot section of earth, stone, or wood into ramp, bridge, or barricade (hardness equals your proficiency bonus; HP equals 5 × your proficiency bonus). The shaping lasts 1 minute or until destroyed.
- **14th Level — Summer’s Dominion**. Allies within 10 feet of you gain a +1 bonus to AC and have advantage on Strength saving throws and ability checks made to resist being shoved or knocked prone. Enemies that start their turn within 10 feet of you have their speed reduced by 10 feet until the start of your next turn.
- **18th Level — Pride of the Sunshine King**. Your Summer’s Dominion aura extends to 15 feet. Enemy creatures that start their turn in the aura and are benefiting from a spell they cast on themselves (including concentration) take 2d6 radiant damage and have disadvantage on Constitution saving throws made to maintain concentration until the end of their turn. Once on each of its turns, such a creature must succeed on a Constitution saving throw against your spell save DC or one such effect of your choice on it ends. A creature can be affected by this feature no more than once on each of its turns.

### Xolturasus — Edge of the Absolute
A feline of winter who conceived the very idea of cold, favoring peaks, chasms, and tundra. Many first hear her call in dreams and feel most attuned at night. Xolturasus invokers are arcane assassins who isolate targets, nullify defenses, and strike in the hush of blizzards, cutting away reactions, movement, and spellcasting before an enemy can respond.

- **Proficiencies** You gain proficiency with the rapier, dagger and longbow.

- **Hallmark Spells** Your bloodline adds these to your known options as you gain levels (they count as Invoker spells for you):
  - 1st: ice knife, armor of Agathys
  - 2nd: hold person, silence
  - 3rd: sleet storm, slow
  - 4th: greater invisibility, ice storm
  - 5th: cone of cold, wall of force (crystalline sheen)
- **1st Level — Resonant Whiteout**. When you cast an invoker spell, you can spend Resonance to apply one of the following instead of (or in addition to) a base spend:
  - Mute (1): If the spell targets a creature, that creature can’t cast spells with verbal components and can’t speak until the start of your next turn (Constitution save negates; immune on success until end of your next turn). Creatures that can’t speak a language are unaffected.
  - Shackle (1): If the spell damages a creature, it can’t take reactions until the start of your next turn and its speed is reduced by 10 feet (no save). If the creature has no ally within 10 feet, the speed reduction is 20 feet instead.
  - Whiteout (1): Create a 10‑foot‑radius swirling snow centered on a point you can see within 60 feet until the start of your next turn. The area is lightly obscured to allies and heavily obscured to enemies. Enemies that enter the area for the first time on a turn or start their turn there must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or have disadvantage on Perception checks and attack rolls until the start of their next turn.
  - Absolute Aim (1): Add +2 to one spell attack roll you make this turn; if the target has no ally within 10 feet, you also gain advantage on that roll.
- **2nd Level — Veilwalker**. You gain proficiency in Stealth. When you cast an illusion, darkness, or cold spell, you can become invisible until the start of your next turn or until you attack or cast another spell. While invisible from this feature, your movement does not provoke opportunity attacks.
- **3rd Level — Echo of Night**. Your Echo gains a climb speed of 30 feet and advantage on Stealth checks. When you spend Resonance on Mute or Shackle, the Echo can take the Hide action as part of that spend. When you spend Resonance on Whiteout, you can place the center of the effect on the Echo’s space.
- **6th Level — Absolute Zero**. When a creature succeeds on a saving throw against your spell by 2 or less, you can turn the result into a failure (once per short or long rest). If the creature is isolated, you can instead use this feature even if it succeeded by up to 4.
- **10th Level — Stillness Cuts**. When you hit a creature with a spell attack or it fails a save against your spell, you can impose one of the following until the end of its next turn (no save): disadvantage on the next attack roll it makes, or its speed is reduced by 10 feet. If the creature is unable to take reactions (from Shackle or otherwise), it also takes cold damage equal to your proficiency bonus.
- **14th Level — Killing Frost**. As an action, you create a 20‑foot‑radius blizzard centered on a point you can see within 60 feet that lasts for 1 minute and moves with that point. The area is heavily obscured for enemies. The first time on a turn a hostile creature in the area makes an attack, casts a spell, or moves 10 feet or more, it must succeed on a Constitution saving throw against your spell save DC or suffer one of the following of your choice: take 2d6 cold damage and have its speed reduced to 0 until the start of its next turn; or be unable to take reactions until the start of its next turn. A creature can be affected by this feature no more than once per turn. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest, or you can expend 2 Resonance when you cast a spell to use it again.
- **18th Level — Perfect Execution**. Critical hits from your spell attacks occur on a roll of 19–20. When you deal cold damage to a creature that is unable to take reactions or has its speed reduced to 0, add 2d6 additional cold damage. If the creature is both unable to take reactions and has its speed reduced to 0, you instead add 3d6 additional cold damage.

### Sammaelon — Heart of the Storm
A great bird‑like creature of thunder and lightning, free‑spirited and clever, birthing storms wherever he roams. Many feel his first call as a shock during intense emotion or strain — Sammaelon invokers call this "the spark". Sammaelon invokers are passionate, crafty and highly-mobile offensive casters who wield lightning, storms, speed, and illusions to outpace and overwhelm foes.

- **Proficiencies** You gain proficiency with the longsword, halberd and repeating crossbow.

- **Hallmark Spells** Your bloodline adds these to your known options as you gain levels (they count as Invoker spells for you):
  - 1st: zephyr strike, witch bolt
  - 2nd: gust of wind, mirror image
  - 3rd: call lightning, haste
  - 4th: storm sphere, freedom of movement
  - 5th: destructive wave (lightning/thunder), far step
- **1st Level — Resonant Tempest**. When you cast an invoker spell, you can spend Resonance to apply one of the following instead of (or in addition to) a base spend:
  - Flash (1): Teleport up to 10 feet to a space you can see. If you dealt lightning or thunder damage with the spell, you can instead teleport 20 feet.
  - Booming Step (1): Push a creature affected by the spell up to 10 feet. If it collides with a creature or object, it takes extra damage equal to your Charisma modifier (lightning or thunder, your choice).
  - Static Veil (1): Until the start of your next turn, you gain +1 AC and become blurred; opportunity attacks against you are made with disadvantage.
  - Eye of the Storm (2): Create a 10‑foot‑radius zone centered on a point you can see within 60 feet until the start of your next turn: enemies treat it as difficult terrain and have disadvantage on ranged attack rolls; the first time an enemy enters, it takes lightning damage equal to your proficiency bonus.
- **2nd Level — Stormmind**. Opportunity attacks against you are made with disadvantage. When you cast a spell that deals lightning or thunder damage, you can teleport to an unoccupied space you can see within 10 feet of a creature damaged by the spell.
- **3rd Level — Echo of the Gale**. Your Echo gains a fly speed equal to its speed but must end its turn on a surface or begin falling. When you spend Resonance on Flash or Eye of the Storm, the Echo can move up to 10 feet.
- **6th Level — Sky’s Reprisal**. When a creature you can see within 60 feet deals damage to you or an ally, you can spend 1 Resonance as a reaction to strike it with rebounding thunder, dealing lightning or thunder damage equal to your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier.
- **10th Level — Ride the Bolt**. When you cast a spell of 1st level or higher, you can fly until the end of your current turn. If you start your turn airborne, you have advantage on the first spell attack you make that turn.
- **14th Level — Storm Sovereign**. As an action, create a 20‑foot‑radius storm eye centered on a point you can see within 90 feet for 1 minute. Choose up to two effects active while it persists: heavy wind (ranged attacks disadvantaged), crackling field (enemies take 1d6 lightning when they start their turn in the area), booming steps (allies in the area gain +10 movement and can move through enemies’ spaces). You can change one effect as a bonus action each turn. Use once per long rest or by expending 2 Resonance when you cast a spell.
- **18th Level — Thunderhead Ascendant**. You gain a fly speed of 60 feet and hover. Your lightning and thunder spells ignore resistance, and when you roll damage for such a spell, you can reroll any number of the dice once.
