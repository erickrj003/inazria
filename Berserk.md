## Berserker — Branded Fury

In Inazria, Berserkers are warriors marked by a living brand that binds them to ancient powers and to their kin. The brand grants overwhelming strength at a cost, pushing the Berserker to the edge of self and sanity. As with all classes in Inazria, your chosen context supplies the majority of your identity and abilities; the base class provides the core engine.

### Hit Points
- **Hit Dice**: 1d12 per berserker level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level**: 12 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels**: 1d12 (or 7) + your Constitution modifier per berserker level after 1st

### Proficiencies
- **Armor**: Light armor, medium armor, shields
- **Weapons**: Simple weapons, martial weapons
- **Tools**: None
- **Saving Throws**: Strength, Constitution
- **Skills**: Choose two from Animal Handling, Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, Survival

### Equipment
You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:
- (a) any martial melee weapon
- (a) two handaxes or (b) any simple weapon
- An explorer’s pack and four javelins

### Class Rules
- **Ongoing Features and Concentration**. If a feature creates a lasting zone, form, or summoned ally, you concentrate on it as if concentrating on a spell, unless the feature says otherwise.
- **Once on Each of Your Turns**. When a feature could trigger multiple times in a round (such as an aura or additional effect), it can do so no more than once on each of your turns unless the feature says otherwise.
- **Limited Uses**. If a feature has a limited number of uses and doesn’t specify a recharge, you can use it a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

### Core Class Features
- **The Brand of the Berserker (1st Level)**. You bear a supernatural mark placed by a malevolent power, linking your fate to your bloodline. The brand cannot be removed by normal means and manifests uniquely upon your body.
- **Berserk (1st Level)**. As a bonus action, you enter a berserk state that lasts for 1 minute. It ends early if you are knocked unconscious or if your turn ends and you haven’t attacked a hostile creature since your last turn and haven’t taken damage since then. While berserking:
  - You have advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws.
  - When you hit with a melee weapon attack using Strength, you add a bonus to the damage roll equal to **half your proficiency bonus (rounded up)**. This bonus applies **once on each of your turns**.
  - You have resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage.
  - Brand Surge. Once on each of your turns when you hit with a melee attack, you can trigger a minor surge shaped by your context’s identity (detailed in each context). A target can be affected by your Brand Surge only once per turn.
  While berserking, you can’t concentrate on casting or preparing spells. When your berserk ends, you must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier). On a failed save, you lose consciousness and fall prone for 1 minute. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Kinship Bond (2nd Level)**. Your brand resonates with others who bear it, strengthening your pack in and out of battle.
  - Branded Sense. You can sense the general direction and distance of your branded kin within 1 mile, and you can communicate telepathically with them within 100 feet.
  - Shared Vigor. When a branded ally you can see within 30 feet takes damage, you can use your reaction to take half of that damage instead (rounded down). You can use this reaction a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.
  - Context Resonance. When you and at least one branded ally of the **same context** both hit the same creature in a round, you invoke a resonance effect unique to that context (see each context for “Resonance” below). A given creature can be affected by your resonance only once per round.
  - Rite of the Brand (Out of Combat). During a short rest, you and up to five branded allies can perform a rite: you share memories and scars, learning one meaningful detail about a creature or place you all encountered within the last day (DM’s choice of insight). In addition, each participant gains advantage on one ability check of their choice before the next long rest. This rite can be performed once per long rest.
- **Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th Levels)**. Increase one ability score by 2, or two ability scores by 1 (to a maximum of 20).
- **Extra Attack (5th Level)**. You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.
- **Berserker Mastery (13th Level)**. Your brand refines your brutality. While berserking:
  - Brutal Follow‑Through. Once on each of your turns, when you hit with a melee attack, you can deal extra damage equal to one of the weapon’s damage dice.
  - Sundering Mark. When you score a critical hit with a melee attack, the target has disadvantage on the next saving throw it makes before the end of your next turn.
- **Avatar (20th Level)**. Your Strength and Constitution scores each increase by 4. Your maximum for those scores increases to 24. You can enter and end your berserk state freely without expending uses. Your berserk still ends if you are knocked unconscious.

## Contexts

### Gibboramites — Rot’s Embrace
Driven from the plains into fetid swamps, the Gibboramites embrace decay as a sign of favor from Gibbora. Their brands resemble sickly green lines that darken with battle experience.

- **Brand Surge — Rotbite**. When you trigger Brand Surge on a hit, the target takes an extra 1d4 necrotic damage and can’t regain hit points until the start of your next turn (once/turn).
- **Resonance — Miasmic Choir**. If you and a Gibboramite ally both hit the same creature in a round, that creature must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or be poisoned until the end of your next turn.
- **Swamp’s Embrace (1st Level)**. You have resistance to poison damage and advantage on saving throws against being poisoned. While berserking, your melee weapon attacks deal an extra 1 necrotic damage, and when a creature hits you with a melee attack, it must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or be poisoned until the end of its next turn.
- **Bog Walker (2nd Level)**. You ignore difficult terrain caused by marsh, mud, or natural decay. You can hold your breath for minutes equal to 5 + your Constitution modifier. You have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made in swamp, marsh, or forest environments, and you gain proficiency with the herbalism kit.
- **Withering Touch (3rd Level)**. When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack while berserking, you can inflict a withering curse (no action required). The target must make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier). On a failed save, it takes 1d4 necrotic damage at the start of each of its turns for a number of rounds equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum 1). Affected creatures can repeat the save at the end of each of their turns, ending the effect on a success. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum 1), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Putrid Resilience (6th Level)**. You are immune to poison damage and the poisoned condition. When you take necrotic damage, you can use your reaction to regain hit points equal to half the damage taken (minimum of 1). You can use this reaction once on each of your turns.
- **Death Sight (7th Level)**. You can see normally in darkness, both magical and nonmagical, out to 60 feet. As an action, you can sense the presence and location of undead within 60 feet of you for 1 minute. Once you use this action, you can’t use it again until you finish a short rest.
- **Aura of Decay (9th Level)**. While berserking, hostile creatures within 10 feet of you have disadvantage on Constitution saving throws. When a hostile creature within this aura is reduced to 0 hit points, you regain hit points equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum 1).
- **Enhanced Withering Touch (10th Level)**. Your withering touch damage increases to 1d6, and the save DC increases by 2. In addition, you can cast detect poison and disease at will.
- **Spore Cloud (11th Level)**. As an action, you release toxic spores in a 15‑foot‑radius sphere centered on yourself. Each hostile creature in the area must make a Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 2d8 poison damage and is poisoned for 1 minute. On a successful save, it takes half damage and isn’t poisoned. A poisoned creature can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a short or long rest.
- **Corrosive Presence (14th Level)**. While berserking, your attacks against objects and structures deal double damage. When a creature hits you with a nonmagical weapon, that weapon takes a permanent −1 penalty to damage rolls (or −1 to AC for natural weapons/unarmed strikes). If a penalty reduces an item to −5, it is destroyed. Magic items are immune.
- **Champion of Rot (15th Level)**. While berserking, you have resistance to necrotic damage; your Swamp’s Embrace damage bonus increases to 2d4 per melee weapon attack; and your aura of decay radius increases to 15 feet. Hostile creatures that start their turn in your aura take 1 necrotic damage.
- **Domain of Blight (17th Level)**. As an action, you transform a 100‑foot‑radius area into corrupted swampland for **10 minutes**. The area is difficult terrain for hostile creatures; you have advantage on attack rolls within it; and hostile creatures that start their turns in the area take 1d4 poison damage. The transformation also creates pools of stagnant water and twisted vegetation that **persist for 24 hours** after the domain ends; during that time, the area kills ordinary crops and small animals (Tiny or smaller) that enter it. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Lord of Decay (18th Level)**. While berserking, every melee hit automatically applies your withering touch (no uses required). Once per long rest, you can assume the mantle of Gibbora for 1 minute: you are immune to all damage except radiant and force; hostile creatures that start their turn within 15 feet of you take 2d6 necrotic damage; creatures that die from necrotic damage you deal can’t be resurrected by spells of 6th level or lower; your form corrodes objects you pass through as if moving through difficult terrain. Magical weapons and armor are affected by Corrosive Presence; touching nonmagical weapons or armor immediately reduces them to −5.

### Bashani — Tides of the Deep
Masters of cold darkness and crushing pressure, the Bashani train in flooded caverns beneath the Veldt’s roots.

- **Brand Surge — Undertow**. When you trigger Brand Surge on a hit, you can pull the target up to 5 feet toward you and reduce its speed by 5 feet until the start of your next turn.
- **Resonance — Riptide Lock**. If you and a Bashani ally both hit the same creature in a round, that creature must succeed on a Strength saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or be knocked prone or grappled by you (your choice) until the end of your next turn.
- **Deep Current (1st Level)**. You have a swimming speed equal to your walking speed, can hold your breath for minutes equal to 15 + your Constitution modifier, and have resistance to cold damage. While berserking, an area within 5 feet of you counts as difficult terrain for hostile creatures, and your melee attacks deal an extra 1 cold damage.
- **Tidal Instincts (2nd Level)**. You have advantage on initiative rolls and can’t be surprised while conscious. You gain proficiency in Athletics (or expertise if already proficient). Your walking speed increases by 5 feet.
- **Crushing Grasp (3rd Level)**. While berserking, when you hit with a melee attack, you can attempt to grapple the target as a bonus action. A creature grappled by you takes 1d4 bludgeoning damage at the start of its turn from crushing pressure. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a short or long rest.
- **Pressure Adaptation (6th Level)**. You have resistance to bludgeoning damage. While berserking, you can’t be knocked prone by effects of your level or lower, and forced movement against you is reduced by 10 feet (minimum 0).
- **Abyssal Sight (7th Level)**. Your darkvision increases to 120 feet, and you can see normally in magical darkness within 60 feet. You can sense the location of any creature within 30 feet that is touching the same body of water as you.
- **Tidal Surge (9th Level)**. As an action, you unleash a crushing wave in a 20‑foot cone. Each creature in the area must make a Strength saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier). On a failed save, a creature is knocked prone and takes 2d6 bludgeoning damage; on a successful save, it takes half damage and isn’t knocked prone. The area becomes difficult terrain for 1 minute. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Deep Current Strike (10th Level)**. When you hit with a melee attack while berserking, you can push the target up to 15 feet. If it collides with a solid obstacle, it takes an extra 1d6 bludgeoning damage. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a short or long rest.
- **Leviathan’s Resilience (11th Level)**. You have immunity to cold damage. While berserking, when you take damage, you can use your reaction to create a shockwave that targets one creature within 15 feet; that creature must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or be stunned until the end of its next turn. Once you use this reaction, you can’t use it again until you finish a short or long rest.
- **Oceanic Domain (14th Level)**. As an action, you create a 20‑foot‑radius zone of crushing pressure centered on yourself for 1 minute. Hostile creatures in the area have their speed halved and take 1d6 bludgeoning damage at the start of their turns. The zone moves with you. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Champion of the Deep (15th Level)**. While berserking, your reach increases by 5 feet, your melee attacks deal an extra 1d4 cold damage, you can breathe water and air equally, and you have tremorsense out to 10 feet while you and a target are touching the same body of water.
- **Master of Depths (17th Level)**. Once per long rest, you can channel abyssal power for 1 minute. You move through water as if it were air and have a swim speed of 60 feet. As an action during this minute, you can unleash a pressurized water blast in a 40‑foot line, 5 feet wide (Dexterity save for half), dealing 3d8 bludgeoning damage on a failed save.
- **Improved Oceanic Domain (18th Level)**. Your domain expands to a 40‑foot radius and lasts 1 minute. The area is heavily obscured for enemies; their speed is halved; they take 1d6 cold + 1d6 bludgeoning damage at the start of their turns; and fire damage dealt in the area is halved. If a creature is knocked prone or pushed outside your domain, it is stunned during its next turn. You can use this feature twice per long rest.

### Asurani — Sky’s Wrath
High above the Veldt’s canopy, the Asurani seek elevation and light, channeling fiery ascent.

- **Brand Surge — Sunlance**. When you trigger Brand Surge on a hit, the target takes an extra 1d4 fire or radiant damage (your choice when you surge) and sheds dim light in a 5‑foot radius until the start of your next turn; it can’t benefit from being invisible during that time.
- **Resonance — Zenith Strike**. If you and an Asurani ally both hit the same creature in a round, you can force it to make a Dexterity saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength or Constitution modifier). On a failed save, its speed becomes 0 until the start of your next turn as solar winds pin it in place.
- **Skybound Heritage (1st Level)**. You have a climbing speed equal to your walking speed and resistance to fire damage. You take no damage from falls of 20 feet or less and reduce any fall damage you take by an amount equal to five times your berserker level. While berserking, your weapons shed bright light in a 10‑foot radius (dim light 10 feet), and your melee attacks deal an extra 1 fire damage.
- **Aerial Grace (2nd Level)**. You gain proficiency in Acrobatics (or expertise if already proficient). You have advantage on saving throws against being knocked prone, and your long jump distance is tripled while your high jump distance is doubled.
- **Blazing Assault (3rd Level)**. While berserking, when you score a critical hit with a melee attack, you can set the target ablaze (no action required). The target takes 1d6 fire damage at the start of each of its turns until it, or another creature within 5 feet of it, uses an action to extinguish the flames. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.
- **Flame Wings (6th Level)**. While berserking, ethereal wings of fire manifest. You gain a fly speed of 25 feet, but you must end your turn on solid ground or begin falling. If you attack a creature while flying above it, you deal an extra 1d4 fire damage on the hit.
- **Solar Sight (7th Level)**. Bright light doesn’t impose disadvantage on your Wisdom (Perception) checks, and you can see clearly up to twice the normal range in bright light. You can perceive invisible creatures within 30 feet as solar silhouettes: attacks you make against such a creature don’t suffer disadvantage from its invisibility, and attacks it makes against you don’t gain advantage from its invisibility. The creature remains invisible to others as normal.
- **Diving Strike (9th Level)**. When you move at least 20 feet straight toward a target and hit it with a melee attack, you deal an extra 1d8 damage, and the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength modifier) or be knocked prone. If you were flying, the extra damage increases to 2d8. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per short or long rest.
- **Flame Mastery (10th Level)**. You have immunity to fire damage. Your Blazing Assault damage increases to 1d8, and you can cast burning hands at 3rd level once per short rest (Constitution is your spellcasting ability).
- **Solar Flare (11th Level)**. As an action, you release blinding radiance in a 20‑foot radius centered on yourself. Each hostile creature in the area must make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or be blinded until the end of its next turn and take 2d6 radiant damage (half damage and not blinded on a success). Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a short or long rest.
- **Meteor (14th Level)**. As an action, you launch yourself up to 80 feet to a point you can see. Each creature within 15 feet of your landing point must make a Dexterity saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier), taking 5d8 fire damage on a failed save, or half as much on a success. The area becomes difficult terrain for 1 minute due to smoldering embers. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Champion of the Sky (15th Level)**. While berserking, you have a fly speed of 40 feet and can hover; you are immune to fire damage; your melee attacks have 5 feet of additional reach; and you shed bright light in a 15‑foot radius.
- **Lord of the Burning Sky (17th Level)**. Your fly speed increases to 60 feet, and you can carry one willing creature of your size or smaller while flying at full speed. You can cast fireball at 6th level twice per long rest without expending spell slots (Constitution is your spellcasting ability).
- **Celestial Ascension (18th Level)**. Once per long rest, you enter an angelic state for 1 minute. You shed bright light in a 30‑foot radius (dim light 30 feet), have resistance to all damage except cold and necrotic, and can use a bonus action to teleport up to 60 feet, dealing 1d8 fire damage to all creatures within 5 feet of both your departure and arrival points. Your weapon attacks deal an extra 1d6 radiant damage while this state lasts.

### Orashkans — Bestial Corruption
Nomads of the badlands, the Orashkans wear corruption like armor and become living monstrosities when the brand flares.

- **Brand Surge — Predator’s Tear**. When you trigger Brand Surge on a hit, you deal an extra 1d4 slashing, piercing, or bludgeoning damage (your choice when you surge) and can move 5 feet without provoking opportunity attacks.
- **Resonance — Pack Break**. If you and an Orashkan ally both hit the same creature in a round, that creature must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or be frightened of you until the start of your next turn.
- **Predator’s Instincts (2nd Level)**. You have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on hearing or smell, and you can track creatures by scent if you move at half speed. You gain proficiency in Survival (or expertise if already proficient). Your walking speed increases by 5 feet.
- **Bestial Transformation (3rd Level)**. While berserking, you undergo a partial transformation into a monstrous creature resembling a beast chosen when you gain this feature (such as wolf, bear, or boar). You grow natural weapons that deal 1d4 + your Strength modifier slashing, piercing, or bludgeoning damage (your choice on each hit). These count as magical weapons. As a bonus action, you can make an attack with these natural weapons.
- **Adaptive Mutation (3rd Level)**. When you take acid, cold, fire, lightning, or thunder damage, you gain resistance to that damage type until you finish a long rest. During a long rest, you can adapt further, choosing one: resistance to extreme heat or cold for 24 hours, the ability to breathe underwater for 24 hours, or advantage on Constitution saving throws against disease and poison for 24 hours.
- **Pack Leader (6th Level)**. You can cast animal friendship once per long rest without expending a spell slot, but only targeting beasts. While berserking, when a beast or monstrosity with Intelligence 4 or lower starts its turn within 30 feet of you, you can use your reaction to force it to make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier). On a failed save, it is charmed by you until the start of its next turn.
- **Apex Senses (7th Level)**. You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened or charmed. Your natural weapons deal an extra 1 damage, and you gain darkvision out to 60 feet (or increase existing darkvision by 30 feet).
- **Territorial Dominance (9th Level)**. As an action, you mark an 80‑foot‑radius area as your hunting ground for 10 minutes. Within the area, you have advantage on attack rolls and Dexterity (Stealth) checks, and the first time on each of your turns that you deal damage to a hostile creature in the area, it takes an extra 1d4 psychic damage. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a short or long rest.
- **Evolved Predator (10th Level)**. Your natural weapon damage increases to 1d6. When you attack with your natural weapons as a bonus action, you can make two such attacks instead of one.
- **Rapid Regeneration (11th Level)**. At the start of your turn, if you have fewer than half your hit points and haven’t taken radiant damage since your last turn, you regain hit points equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum 1).
- **Call of the Wild (14th Level)**. You can cast conjure animals at 5th level once per long rest without expending a spell slot. The summoned creatures appear slightly corrupted and gain resistance to one damage type of your choice. They obey your commands and last for 10 minutes.
- **Champion of Monstrosity (15th Level)**. While berserking, you become an apex horror:
  - Your size increases by one category (Small to Medium, Medium to Large, etc.); worn equipment grows with you as normal for enlarge.
  - Bestial Carapace. Your natural armor equals **13 + your Constitution modifier** (you can’t benefit from armor while this is active). You can still benefit from a shield.
  - Your Strength score increases by 2 (this can exceed 20 but not 24).
- **Orashka’s Madness (17th Level)**. Once per long rest, you embrace primal corruption for 1 minute. During this time:
  - Your melee weapon and natural weapon attacks have advantage.
  - Your natural weapon attacks deal maximum damage.
  - You have resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage.
  - Compulsion: You must use your action each turn to attack the nearest creature (friendly or hostile). You choose which attacks to make and can use your abilities. When the effect ends, you gain one level of exhaustion.
- **Improved Call of the Wild (18th Level)**. Once per long rest, you summon more powerful beasts. Your Call of the Wild functions as conjure animals cast at 8th level, and the creatures gain resistances to two damage types of your choice. You can use this feature twice per long rest.
