## Druid — Masters of Natural Power

The Druid channels the will of the natural world—spirits, elements, and living memory—into decisive magic and battlefield presence. In Inazria, each Druid chooses a context that reflects regional, cultural, or ancestral tradition. Roughly a quarter of a Druid’s power comes from the base class; the rest is granted by the chosen context.

### Hit Points
- **Hit Dice**: 1d8 per druid level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level**: 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels**: 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per druid level after 1st

### Proficiencies
- **Armor**: Light armor, medium armor, shields
- **Weapons**: Simple weapons, martial weapons
- **Tools**: Herbalism kit
- **Saving Throws**: Wisdom, Intelligence
- **Skills**: Choose two from Arcana, Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Religion, Survival

### Equipment
You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:
- (a) a wooden shield or (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a scimitar or (b) any simple melee weapon
- Leather armor, an explorer’s pack, and a druid focus (crystal, staff, or natural item)

### Spellcasting
- The Druid table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your druid spells of 1st level and higher. To cast one of these spells, you must expend a slot of the spell’s level or higher. You regain all expended spell slots when you finish a long rest.
- You know druid spells based on your level and your chosen context. Each context provides its own spell list and progression.
- Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for druid spells.
  - **Spell save DC** = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier
  - **Spell attack modifier** = your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier
- You can cast any druid spell you know as a ritual if that spell has the ritual tag.
- You can use a druid focus as a spellcasting focus for your druid spells.

### Class Rules
- **Ongoing Features and Concentration**. If a feature creates a lasting zone, form, or summoned ally, you concentrate on it as if concentrating on a spell, unless the feature says otherwise. Your concentration ends as normal, and the feature ends when your concentration ends.
- **Once on Each of Your Turns**. When a feature could trigger multiple times in a round (such as an aura or additional effect), it can do so no more than once on each of your turns unless the feature says otherwise.
- **Limited Uses**. If a feature has a limited number of uses and doesn’t specify a recharge, you can use it a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Summons and Companions**. A creature you summon with a class feature acts on your initiative, immediately after your turn. On your turn, you can use a bonus action to command any or all creatures you control from that feature to take the Attack, Dash, Disengage, Help, or Hide action, or another action that feature allows. If you don’t issue a command, a summoned creature takes the Dodge action and moves to avoid danger. Unless a feature states otherwise, you can have only one instance of that summoning feature active at a time, and the summoning requires your concentration.

### Core Class Features
- **Druidic (1st Level)**. You know Druidic, the secret language of druids. You can speak it and leave hidden messages. Druids automatically spot such messages; others spot them with a successful DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check but can’t decipher them without magic.
- **Natural Affinity (1st Level)**. As an action, you can converse with a beast for 10 minutes. The beast has no obligation to be helpful or truthful. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest. In addition, once per long rest when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, you instead immediately regain hit points equal to your druid level + your Wisdom modifier. Your damage resistance is determined by your context; the base class doesn’t grant resistance beyond that.
- **Wild Empathy (2nd Level)**. As an action, you magically influence beasts and plants within 30 feet. Choose up to a number of such creatures equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of one). Each target must make a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed save, it is charmed by you for 1 minute or until it takes damage. While charmed, it won’t attack you or your companions, though it has no obligation to help you. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th Levels)**. Increase one ability score by 2, or two ability scores by 1 (to a maximum of 20).
- **Natural Recovery (6th Level)**. During a short rest, you can recover expended spell slots with a combined level equal to or less than half your druid level (rounded up). None of the slots can be 6th level or higher. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Timeless Body (18th Level)**. For every 10 years that pass, your body ages only 1 year. You are immune to magical aging.
- **Archdruid (20th Level)**. You can use Wild Empathy at will. You can ignore the verbal and somatic components of your druid spells and any material components that lack a cost and aren’t consumed by the spell. When you roll initiative, you regain all expended uses of your context features that recharge on a long rest.

## Contexts

### Channeler (Magi of Old)
“‘The old ways are the most powerful, but power demands sacrifice. The earth itself flows through me, and I am but its vessel.’ Channelers are living conduits for the raw, untamed forces of Azergad. They amplify magic through symbiosis with the world, embracing risk to unleash overwhelming might.”

#### Spell Progression
- **Cantrips Known**: 2 at 1st level; +1 at 4th and 10th levels
- **Spells Known**: 4 at 1st level; +2 per level to 9th; +1 per level thereafter

#### Context Features
- **Terrestrial Conduit (1st Level)**. After each long rest, choose one element: Stone (bludgeoning), Flame (fire), Frost (cold), Storm (lightning), Corrosion (acid), Resonance (thunder), Thorn (piercing), Toxin (poison), or Sunlight (radiant). You gain resistance to the associated damage type. You can sense the presence of your chosen element within 1 mile (for example, caverns for stone, volcanism for flame, or incoming storms for storm). If you finish a long rest in an environment saturated with your chosen element, you regain one additional spell slot of your highest level.
- **Planetary Surge (1st Level)**. When you cast a spell of 1st level or higher, you can channel planetary energy to enhance it. Roll a d6:
  - 1–2. Empowered Force. Add your Wisdom modifier to the spell’s damage. You take psychic damage equal to the spell’s level.
  - 3–4. Extended Reach. The spell’s range is doubled. Until the start of your next turn, you have disadvantage on your next attack roll or ability check.
  - 5–6. Widened Effect. The spell affects one additional target within range. You can’t take reactions until the start of your next turn.
  You can use Planetary Surge a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Environmental Mastery (3rd Level)**. When you cast a spell in an environment resonant with your chosen element, choose one of the following options (you must use each option once before you can repeat any of them):
  - Elemental Infusion. If the spell deals damage, you can change its damage type to your chosen element and add your Wisdom modifier to one damage roll of the spell.
  - Environmental Overcast. You cast the spell at one level higher without expending a higher slot, then make a Constitution saving throw (DC = 10 + the spell’s original level + 1). On a failed save, you take necrotic damage equal to twice the spell’s original level + 2.
  - Planetary Resonance. The spell’s save DC increases by 2, then you make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 15). On a failed save, you are overwhelmed for 1 minute and must use your action each turn to cast a cantrip at the nearest creature as the world acts through you.
  You can use Environmental Mastery a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Vessel of the World (5th Level)**. As a bonus action, you enter a Terrestrial Trance that lasts for up to 1 minute. The trance ends early if your concentration is broken (as if concentrating on a spell). When the trance begins, choose two of the three aspects of your chosen element (see Element Effects below). While in the trance, when you deal damage of your chosen type, you add damage equal to your Wisdom modifier. At the start of each of your turns, roll a d4; on a 1, Azergad compels you to cast a cantrip at the nearest creature.
  You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Vessel of the World: Element Effects**
  - Stone (Bludgeoning). Earthen Resilience. You have resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage from nonmagical attacks. Tremor Step. As you move, you can create a 5‑foot radius of difficult terrain along your path until the start of your next turn. Crushing Force. Your spell attacks and damaging spells knock targets prone on a critical hit or when a target fails its save by 5 or more.
  - Flame (Fire). Ignition Aura. When a creature starts its turn within 5 feet of you, you can cause it to take 1d4 fire damage (no more than once per creature per turn). Blazing Speed. Your speed increases by 10 feet, and opportunity attacks against you have disadvantage. Cauterizing Magic. When your spells deal fire damage, you regain hit points equal to half the damage dealt (maximum per spell equal to your Wisdom modifier).
  - Frost (Cold). Glacial Armor. You gain temporary hit points equal to your druid level at the start of each of your turns while the trance lasts. Freezing Touch. A creature you touch—or that hits you with a melee attack—must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier) or have its speed reduced by 10 feet until the end of its next turn. Ice Shards. When you take damage, you can use your reaction to deal cold damage equal to your Wisdom modifier to the attacker.
  - Storm (Lightning). Lightning Reflexes. You can take one additional reaction each round, and your initiative increases by your Wisdom modifier. Chain Lightning. When your spells deal lightning damage to a creature, the damage arcs to one other creature within 15 feet for half damage. Storm Step. As a bonus action, you can teleport up to 30 feet, leaving a crackling trail that deals lightning damage equal to your Wisdom modifier to one creature you pass through (once per turn).
  - Corrosion (Acid). Acid Blood. When you take piercing or slashing damage, the attacker takes acid damage equal to your proficiency bonus (once per turn). Dissolving Aura. Your spells ignore resistance to acid damage; creatures with immunity to acid instead have resistance. Corrosive Magic. A creature that fails its save against your spell has its AC reduced by 1 until it finishes a short rest (maximum reduction of 3).
  - Resonance (Thunder). Harmonic Disruption. Your thunder damage imposes disadvantage on the target’s next attack roll or saving throw (once per turn). Vibration Sense. You have tremorsense out to 30 feet and can’t be blinded or deafened. Shockwave. When you cast a spell, you can create a 10‑foot‑radius shockwave around the target. Each creature in the area must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier) or be knocked prone (once per turn).
  - Thorn (Piercing). Barbed Defense. When a creature hits you with a melee attack, it takes piercing damage equal to your Wisdom modifier and must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier) or be poisoned until the end of its next turn (once per turn). Entangling Growth. Your spells create thorny growth in a 5‑foot radius around their impact point that is difficult terrain and deals 1d4 piercing damage to creatures that start their turn there. Piercing Magic. Your spells that deal piercing damage gain a +2 bonus to attack rolls and spell save DCs.
  - Toxin (Poison). Venomous Aura. When a creature starts its turn within 10 feet of you, it must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier) or be poisoned until the start of its next turn (once per creature per turn). Toxic Immunity. You are immune to poison damage and the poisoned condition, and you can move through poisonous environments without harm. Lingering Poison. A creature that takes poison damage from your spells takes additional poison damage equal to your Wisdom modifier at the start of its next turn (once per creature per turn).
  - Sunlight (Radiant). Healing Light. At the start of each of your turns, you and allies within 10 feet regain hit points equal to your Wisdom modifier (once per turn). Blinding Brilliance. When your radiant spells deal damage to a creature, that creature must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier) or be blinded until the end of its next turn. Purifying Energy. Your radiant spells remove one condition (charmed, frightened, poisoned, or diseased) from targets they heal or from allies in their area of effect.
- **Greater Good (9th Level)**. You can change your chosen element as an action. Additionally, when you cast a spell, you can take necrotic damage equal to three times the spell’s level (this damage can’t be reduced) to cast it without expending a spell slot. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Terrestrial Dominion (11th Level)**. As an action, you create a 30‑foot‑radius World Nexus centered on a point you can see within 120 feet. The nexus lasts for up to 1 minute and requires your concentration. Terrain in the area reflects your element; the area is difficult terrain for creatures without resistance to your chosen damage type. When a creature starts its turn in the area, it takes 2d6 damage of your chosen type (Constitution save against your spell save DC; a successful save halves the damage). The first spell you cast on each of your turns that targets a creature in the area—or that has its point of origin in the area—deals maximum damage. At the start of your turn, you can move the nexus up to 30 feet as a bonus action. When you do so, you must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or be overwhelmed and forced to use your action that turn to maintain the nexus, unable to cast other spells.
- **Planetary Dominance (14th Level)**. You gain immunity to your chosen damage type. When you would take damage of that type, you instead regain hit points equal to half the damage you would have taken. If you finish a short rest in an environment resonant with your element, you regain one expended spell slot of 3rd level or lower. If you finish a long rest in such an environment, the regained slot can be up to 5th level. Once per long rest when Planetary Surge would impose a negative effect on you, you can ignore it, then roll again on the table and accept the second result.
- **Master Channeler (17th Level)**. When you enter your Terrestrial Trance, you can choose all three aspects of your chosen element. In addition, when you deal damage with a spell, you can change the damage type to any element you have previously chosen during this campaign. When you move your World Nexus, you no longer make Wisdom saving throws to maintain control, and you can concentrate on one other spell while the nexus is active. Once per long rest, you can cast any druid spell of 6th level or lower without expending a spell slot. You cast it at 6th level, and if the spell deals damage, it deals damage of your chosen elemental type. The planet itself replaces the spell’s verbal and somatic components.

## Contexts

### Spiritcaller
“‘The ancestors guide my path, and their wisdom flows through me. The spirits of this land answer my call, and together we are unstoppable.’ Spiritcallers bridge the living and the dead, fighting alongside spectral allies while channeling ancient guidance.”

#### Spell Progression
- **Cantrips Known**: 2 at 1st level; +1 at 4th and 10th levels
- **Spells Known**: 3 at 1st level; +1 per level to 5th; +1 every other level thereafter

#### Context Features
- **Ancestral Bond (1st Level)**. Your Natural Affinity manifests as a bond with ancestral spirits. You gain resistance to necrotic damage. You can cast speak with dead once per long rest without expending a spell slot, but only to communicate with spirits you choose to contact. A translucent ancestor of your race accompanies you as an Ancestral Guide. The guide grants the following benefits: you add half your proficiency bonus (rounded up) to any Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma check you make; once per long rest when you fail a saving throw, the guide lets you reroll and use the higher result; and the guide can scout up to 100 feet and report what it observes (10 minutes). Additional benefits by race are as listed previously.
- **Spirit‑Touched Warrior (1st Level)**. You gain proficiency with martial weapons and medium armor. When you make a weapon attack, you can use your Wisdom modifier instead of Strength or Dexterity for the attack and damage rolls, and your weapon attacks count as magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage. Through a 10‑minute ritual, you commune with local spirits to adopt a regional attunement (Ancients, Wild, Lost, Fallen, or Guardians). You can change your attunement when you finish a short rest in a new region.
- **Call to Battle (3rd Level)**. As a bonus action, you enter a Spiritual Trance that lasts for up to 1 minute and requires your concentration. While in the trance, when you take the Attack action on your turn, you can make one additional weapon attack (once on each of your turns). Also, when you hit with a weapon attack, you can push the target up to 10 feet away from you (once on each of your turns). In addition, allies within 10 feet of you gain temporary hit points equal to your Wisdom modifier at the start of their turns. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Spectral Arsenal (5th Level)**. As a bonus action, you manifest a spectral weapon in your hand or hovering within 5 feet of you. The weapon lasts for 1 hour. It functions as any simple or martial weapon of your choice and deals force damage instead of its normal damage type. Once on each of your turns when you hit with the weapon, you can deal an extra 1d6 force damage. If the weapon is hovering, you can use a bonus action to make a ranged spell attack against a creature within 60 feet (on a hit, the target takes 1d6 + your Wisdom modifier force damage). You can have only one spectral weapon at a time.
- **Expanded Spirit Network (9th Level)**. You can change your regional attunement when you finish a short rest and can choose from Elemental Spirits and Artisan Spirits as additional options (as listed previously). Once per long rest, you can cast legend lore without expending a spell slot. During a long rest, you can commune with local spirits to learn one useful fact about recent events (within the past month) that occurred within 1 mile of your location.
- **Spectral Warband (11th Level)**. As an action, you call forth spectral warriors in unoccupied spaces you can see within 30 feet. The warband lasts for up to 1 minute and requires your concentration. The number of warriors equals your proficiency bonus (minimum of one). A warrior has AC 15; hit points equal to 10 + your Wisdom modifier; a speed of 30 feet and hover; and the race tag of your race. A warrior uses your spell attack bonus for its attacks and, on a hit, deals 1d6 + your Wisdom modifier necrotic damage. On your turn, you can use a bonus action to command any or all warriors; each warrior can move up to its speed and make one attack against a creature within 5 feet. If you don’t command the warband, each warrior takes the Help action to aid an ally within 30 feet, or Dodge if it can’t help. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Master of Both Realms (14th Level)**. You can see invisible creatures and objects within 60 feet, and you have truesight out to 30 feet. You have resistance to necrotic and psychic damage, and you are immune to the frightened and charmed conditions. Your spectral arsenal improves: the extra damage becomes 1d8 once on each of your turns, and on a critical hit the target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be frightened until the end of your next turn. You can have two spectral weapons active simultaneously.
- **Lord of Spirits (17th Level)**. When you are reduced to 0 hit points, you can have your ancestral spirit sustain you; you immediately regain hit points equal to half your hit point maximum and gain resistance to all damage until the end of your next turn. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest. When you use Spectral Warband, the number of warriors equals your proficiency bonus + 1, and each warrior has AC 17, resistance to all damage except force, psychic, and radiant, and can make two attacks when commanded. Your guide becomes partially visible to allies and can interact physically, casting any cantrip you know using your spell save DC and spell attack bonus, using the Help action within 30 feet, and making opportunity attacks using your spell attack bonus for 1d8 + your Wisdom modifier force damage. If you die, your guide remains for 24 hours; during that time, any resurrection attempt succeeds regardless of time passed or missing components.

### Beastmaster
“‘The wilderness speaks in a thousand voices, and I have learned to listen. Through fang and claw, through keen eye and swift wing, nature’s will is done.’ Beastmasters weave themselves into the living tapestry, fighting with—and as—the apex.”

#### Spell Progression
- **Cantrips Known**: 2 at 1st level; +1 at 4th and 10th levels
- **Spells Known**: 3 at 1st level; +1 per level to 5th; +1 every other level thereafter

#### Context Features
- **Beast Senses (1st Level)**. When you finish a long rest, choose one sensory adaptation (Predator’s Eyes; Pack Hunter’s Nose; Night Stalker’s Ears; Burrower’s Tremor; Aquatic Instincts) and gain its benefits and resistance until you choose a different adaptation on a later long rest.
- **Animal Allies (1st Level)**. As an action, you summon one creature from the Animal Allies list into an unoccupied space you can see within 30 feet. The creature appears for up to 10 minutes. The summoning requires your concentration. The creature acts on your initiative immediately after your turn and follows the Summons and Companions rules. If you don’t command it, it Dodges and moves to avoid danger. You can have only one Animal Allies summon active at a time. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Primal Coordination (3rd Level)**. Pack Tactics. When you and your Animal Allies creature are both within 5 feet of the same enemy, you both have advantage on attack rolls against that creature. Beast Weaponry. As a bonus action, you manifest natural weapons based on your current Beast Senses for 10 minutes or until you dismiss them (no action). You are proficient with these weapons and use your Wisdom modifier for attack and damage rolls. Each deals 1d6 damage as listed previously. Territorial Awareness. Spend 10 minutes to map hazards and landmarks within 1 mile. For 24 hours, you know the location of all Large or larger creatures within that area and sense when such creatures enter or leave.
- **Apex Hunter (5th Level)**. As a bonus action, you mark a creature you can see within 60 feet as your prey for 1 hour. Until the mark ends, you and creatures you summon with Animal Allies or Elite Animals deal an extra 1d6 damage when you hit the marked creature with a weapon attack, and you have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) and Wisdom (Survival) checks to find it. If the marked creature dies, you can mark a new creature as a bonus action. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest. In addition, when you summon a creature with Animal Allies, it gains temporary hit points equal to your druid level and can use its listed special ability as a bonus action instead of an action. When reduced to 0 hit points, it can make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw; on a success, it drops to 1 hit point instead (once per summoning).
- **Master Tracker (9th Level)**. Dual Senses. You can have two Beast Senses adaptations active simultaneously, gaining both their benefits and resistances. When you finish a long rest, you can change one or both adaptations; when you finish a short rest, you can change one. Elite Animals. When you use Animal Allies, you can choose a creature from the Elite Animals list instead. Tracking Network. You can cast animal messenger at will. You can spend 1 hour establishing a network of Tiny creatures within 5 miles; once per day, you can contact the network to learn about unusual activities, new arrivals, or significant events in the past 24 hours. The network lasts until you establish a new one elsewhere.
- **Legendary Pack (11th Level)**. Pack Leader. When you use Animal Allies, you can summon two creatures using a single use and a single instance of concentration, choosing either two Animal Allies or one Animal Allies creature and one Elite Animal. The second creature begins with half its normal hit points. You command both with the same bonus action. Coordinated Assault. When one of your summoned creatures hits a target, you can use your reaction to command another of your summoned creatures to make one weapon attack against the same target if in range. Evolved Senses and Territory Control are as listed previously.
- **Apex Evolution (14th Level)**. Choose one permanent physical evolution (Alpha Predator; Pack Alpha; Silent Death; Tunnel Lord; Leviathan) tied to a Beast Senses adaptation you have used. Each evolution functions as listed previously. Any active abilities granted by the evolution (such as Pounce, Rally the Pack, Shadowstrike, Earthshaker, or Maelstrom) can be used a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.
- **Lord of Beasts (17th Level)**. You can cast conjure animals once per long rest without expending a spell slot. Creatures summoned by this casting have maximum hit points; add your proficiency bonus to their attack rolls, damage rolls, and saving throws; last for 8 hours; and can communicate with you telepathically at any distance on the same plane (the spell’s concentration rules apply). You permanently gain all five Beast Senses and their Evolved Senses benefits; you can have only two resistances active at a time but can switch between them as a bonus action. You can designate up to three creatures as your long‑term prey; you always know their exact location while they are on the same plane, and you have advantage on attack rolls against them (you can change a designated prey when you finish a long rest). When you use Pack Leader, you can instead choose two Elite Animals, three Animal Allies, or one Elite Animal and two Animal Allies. Once per long rest, you can grant the active benefits of your Apex Evolution to all creatures you have summoned for 1 hour.

#### Animal Allies and Elite Animals
Use the entries as listed previously (Hunting Hawk, Dire Wolf, Giant Spider, Brown Bear, Venomous Snake, Dire Boar, War Hound, Giant Owl; and Shadow Panther, Crystal Fox, Frost Wolf, Sage Owl, Thunderwing Roc, Spectral Dire Bear). Where an entry references actions, it follows the Summons and Companions rules above.

### Astralist
“‘In the dance of stars and the flow of time, I find power beyond mortal understanding.’ Astralists study celestial movement and temporal currents, wielding magic that manipulates space, time, and divine radiance.”

#### Spell Progression
- **Cantrips Known**: 2 at 1st level; +1 at 4th and 10th levels
- **Spells Known**: 3 at 1st level; +1 per level to 5th; +1 every other level thereafter

#### Context Features
- **Stellar Alignment (1st Level)**. Your Natural Affinity manifests as celestial connection. You gain resistance to radiant damage. You can cast guidance and light at will. You always know the time of day, your location, and the direction of true north.
- **Cosmic Awareness (1st Level)**. You have advantage on initiative rolls and can’t be surprised while conscious. Once per long rest, you can ask the cosmos a single question about the future and receive a cryptic but truthful answer (as augury).
- **Temporal Manipulation (3rd Level)**. When you or an ally within 60 feet makes an attack roll or a saving throw, you can use your reaction to allow a reroll, and the creature can use either result. Alternatively, when a creature within 60 feet makes an attack roll, you can use your reaction to force a reroll, and the creature must use the lower result. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Stellar Magic (5th Level)**. When you cast a spell at night or while you can see the sky, choose one of the following options: cast the spell at one level higher without expending a higher slot; have the spell ignore resistance to its damage type; or double the spell’s range. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Dimensional Step (9th Level)**. As a bonus action, you teleport up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space you can see. When you do so, you can bring one willing creature within 5 feet of you to an unoccupied space adjacent to your destination. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Time Dilation (11th Level)**. As an action, you create a 30‑foot‑radius zone centered on yourself that lasts for up to 3 rounds and requires your concentration. Choose one effect when you create the zone:
  - Acceleration. At the start of each of your turns, choose one ally in the zone. That ally gains one additional action on their next turn. A creature can benefit from this only once per round.
  - Deceleration. Creatures hostile to you that are in the zone can take only an action or a bonus action on their turns, not both.
  - Stasis. As part of creating the zone, choose one creature you can see in the zone. It must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC or become incapacitated and have a speed of 0 until the start of your next turn. On a successful save, the creature is unaffected and is immune to your Stasis for 24 hours.
  Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Astral Projection (14th Level)**. As an action, you project your consciousness across space and time for up to 10 minutes, requiring concentration for the duration. Your body becomes unconscious but stable. Your astral form is invisible and incorporeal, has a flying speed of 60 feet, can cast divination spells, and can communicate telepathically with any creature. If your astral form is destroyed, you return to your body with 1 hit point. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Master of Space and Time (17th Level)**. You can cast time stop once per long rest without expending a spell slot. Your Dimensional Step can cross planes of existence. You age at one‑tenth the normal rate. During a long rest, you can see 24 hours into the future, which grants you advantage on the first three d20 rolls you make after the rest.
