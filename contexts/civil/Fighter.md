## Fighter — Master of Arms

In Inazria, Fighters are professionals of violence—soldiers, knights, lancers, and duelists who earned their edge through drill, study, and blood rather than birthright or branding. Where primal warriors draw power from heritage and calling, Fighters assemble capability piece by piece: a fighting style here, a proven technique there, armor and weapon proficiencies worn like the tools of a trade. As with all classes in Inazria, your chosen context supplies the majority of your identity and abilities; the base class provides the core engine.

A Fighter build is intentionally modular. You may **attempt any technique** on the Fighter list, but only techniques you have invested in perform reliably. Breadth is always available; excellence must be earned. That flexibility is the civil tradeoff: adaptability and professional craft instead of the singular, high-ceiling power of a primal path.

### Hit Points
- **Hit Dice**: 1d10 per fighter level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level**: 10 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels**: 1d10 (or 6) + your Constitution modifier per fighter level after 1st

### Proficiencies
- **Armor**: All armor, shields
- **Weapons**: Simple weapons, martial weapons
- **Tools**: None
- **Saving Throws**: Strength, Constitution
- **Skills**: Choose two from Acrobatics, Animal Handling, Athletics, History, Intimidation, Perception, Medicine, and Survival

### Equipment
You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:
- (a) chain mail or (b) leather armor, longbow, and 20 arrows
- (a) a martial weapon and a shield or (b) two martial weapons
- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) two handaxes
- (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack

### Class Rules
- **Ongoing Features and Concentration**. If a feature creates a lasting zone, form, or summoned ally, you concentrate on it as if concentrating on a spell, unless the feature says otherwise.
- **Once on Each of Your Turns**. When a feature could trigger multiple times in a round (such as an aura or additional effect), it can do so no more than once on each of your turns unless the feature says otherwise.
- **Limited Uses**. If a feature has a limited number of uses and doesn't specify a recharge, you can use it a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Multiclassing**. You may take levels in as many civil classes as you wish, up to 30 total character levels. You may have only one primal class. Features from different classes stack unless they say otherwise, but overlapping martial resources (such as a primal Flow engine and Fighter techniques) do not replace one another—you operate both systems independently, and advanced primal abilities may make some civil options less efficient. That friction is intentional.

### Core Class Features
- **Martial Proficiency (1st Level)**. Your training covers the full spectrum of arms and armor. You ignore the Strength requirement of armor you are proficient with. When you attack with a weapon you are proficient with, you can use Strength or Dexterity for the attack and damage rolls (your choice for each attack).
- **Fighting Style (1st Level)**. You adopt a practiced approach to combat. Choose one fighting style from the list below. At 10th level, you learn a second fighting style. Whenever you finish a long rest, you can replace one of your fighting styles with a different option from the list.
- **Exertion (1st Level)**. You can push your body and focus beyond comfortable limits. You have a number of **Exertion** equal to your proficiency bonus. You regain all expended Exertion when you finish a short or long rest.
  - **Second Wind** (1 Exertion, bonus action). You regain hit points equal to 1d10 + your fighter level.
  - **Action Surge** (2 Exertion, on your turn). You take one additional action.
  - **Improvised Technique** (1 Exertion). When you use a technique you are untrained in, you must spend this Exertion (see Combat Techniques).
- **Combat Techniques (1st Level)**. You are exposed to the full catalog of Fighter techniques (see Fighter Techniques). Any technique may be attempted, but only invested techniques work without penalty or Exertion cost.
  - **Technique Points**. You gain **2 technique points** when you take your first fighter level. Each time you gain a fighter level thereafter, you gain 1 additional technique point.
  - **Technique Ranks**. Each technique can be invested to a maximum of three ranks:

    | Rank | Points invested | Effect |
    |---|---|---|
    | Untrained | 0 | You may attempt the technique by spending 1 Exertion; apply the Improvised Use rules below. |
    | Familiar | 1 | The technique works as written in the technique table. You do not spend Exertion to use it. |
    | Practiced | 3 | You gain the Familiar effect and the technique's Practiced benefit. |
    | Mastered | 6 | You gain the Familiar and Practiced effects and the technique's Mastered benefit. |

  - **Improvised Use**. When you use a technique untrained, you must spend 1 Exertion, and the technique suffers all of the following that apply:
    - If the technique includes an attack roll beyond a normal weapon attack granted by the Attack action, that attack roll has disadvantage.
    - If the technique forces a saving throw, the target has advantage on the save.
    - If the technique deals extra damage, grants temporary hit points, or reduces damage, that amount is halved (round down).
    - If the technique normally grants advantage or imposes disadvantage, that part of the technique fails.
  - **Once per Turn**. Unless a technique's Mastered benefit says otherwise, you can use each technique at most once per turn.
  - **Context Techniques**. Techniques granted by your context are added to your available list and follow the same rank rules.
- **Weapon Group Mastery (2nd Level)**. Choose two weapon groups: axes, blades, bludgeons, bows, crossbows, polearms, or thrown weapons. When you hit with a weapon attack using a weapon from a mastered group, you deal extra damage equal to your proficiency bonus. You can deal this extra damage only once per turn.
  - At 6th level, choose a third weapon group. At 13th level, choose a fourth.
- **Veteran's Focus (2nd Level)**. When you finish a short rest, you regain 1 expended Exertion (in addition to the Exertion you normally regain).
- **Tactical Assessment (3rd Level)**. As a bonus action, you study one creature you can see within 30 feet. Until the end of your next turn, you have advantage on attack rolls against that creature, and the first time you hit it with a weapon attack on your turn, you deal extra damage equal to your proficiency bonus. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th Levels)**. Increase one ability score by 2, or two ability scores by 1 (to a maximum of 20).
- **Extra Attack (5th Level)**. When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can attack twice instead of once.
- **Battle Hardened (7th Level)**. You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened. When you fail a saving throw, you can reroll it and must use the new roll. Once you use this reroll, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Indomitable (9th Level)**. When you fail a saving throw, you can reroll it with a bonus equal to your fighter level (maximum +10). You must use the new roll. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Relentless (11th Level)**. When you use Action Surge, you can immediately move up to half your speed without provoking opportunity attacks. When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead. Once you use this feature to cheat death, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.
- **Improved Weapon Mastery (13th Level)**. Your weapon group mastery extra damage increases to twice your proficiency bonus, but you can still deal it only once per turn.
- **Extra Attack (2) (15th Level)**. When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can attack three times instead of twice.
- **Warrior's Reflexes (17th Level)**. You can take a reaction on every turn in combat, instead of only once per round. This reaction can be used for opportunity attacks, techniques, or context features that require a reaction. You can use Action Surge twice before finishing a short or long rest, but only once on the same turn.
- **Martial Legend (20th Level)**. Choose one ability score from Strength, Dexterity, or Constitution. That score increases by 2, to a maximum of 22. You gain 4 technique points to invest immediately. When you roll initiative, if you have 0 Exertion remaining, you gain 1 Exertion.

Unless a Mastered benefit says otherwise, each technique can be used at most once per turn.

**Martial save DC**. Strength saving throws forced by base Fighter techniques use this DC: 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength or Dexterity modifier (your choice when you gain the technique).

## Contexts

Each Fighter context adds techniques to your repertoire (see Fighter Techniques), grants context features at specific fighter levels, and shapes how you apply the base engine. Most characters commit to one Fighter context.

### Soldier — Discipline of the Line

Soldiers are Fighters who learned war as a trade—enlisted, drilled, and deployed until maneuver and endurance replaced instinct. Where a duelist perfects one weapon or a vagabond improvises, a Soldier is graded on kit familiarity, unit cohesion, and the ability to keep others fighting when the line buckles. On the battlefield they read as officers, sergeants, or the reliable veteran who makes a squad into something more than the sum of its parts.

**Philosophy**: Victory through discipline, cohesion, and endurance—the line holds because everyone knows their part.

**Squadmate**. Several Soldier features refer to **squadmates**: allied creatures within 5 feet of you that you can see and that aren't incapacitated. You are never your own squadmate.

- **Arms Drill (1st Level)**. You gain one additional weapon group for **Weapon Group Mastery** (chosen when you reach 2nd level, or immediately if you already have that feature). You can draw or stow a weapon when you move at least 5 feet on your turn (once per turn). Whenever you finish a long rest, you can change which fighting style you know from the Fighter list.
- **Squadmate's Opening (1st Level)**. When a squadmate hits a creature with a weapon attack, you have advantage on the next weapon attack roll you make against that creature before the end of your next turn.
- **Issue Orders (3rd Level)**. As a bonus action, choose one ally within 30 feet who can see or hear you. That ally can immediately take one of the following options (its choice):

  | Order | Effect |
  |---|---|
  | **Press** | Move up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks. |
  | **Strike** | The next attack roll it makes before the end of its next turn has advantage. |
  | **Dig In** | Until the start of its next turn, it gains a +2 bonus to AC and advantage on saving throws against being moved or knocked prone. |

  You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

- **Coordinated Strike (5th Level)**. Once per turn when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, if a squadmate hit that same creature with a weapon attack since the start of your last turn, the hit deals extra damage equal to your proficiency bonus. At 11th level, the extra damage equals twice your proficiency bonus.
- **Campaign Endurance (6th Level)**. You have advantage on saving throws against exhaustion. When you use **Second Wind**, choose one squadmate within 5 feet of you; that ally regains hit points equal to 1d6 + your proficiency bonus.
- **Steady Advance (7th Level)**. Difficult terrain doesn't cost you extra movement. When you move on your turn, each squadmate can move through 5 feet of difficult terrain on its next turn without extra movement cost (this benefit ends at the start of your next turn).
- **Grim Tenacity (9th Level)**. While you have half your hit points or fewer, you have advantage on saving throws. Once per long rest when you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead and regain 1 Exertion.
- **Captain's Presence (11th Level)**. Squadmates within 10 feet of you have advantage on saving throws against being frightened. When you use **Issue Orders**, you can target two allies with the same bonus action.
- **Fortified Position (14th Level)**. While you have at least two squadmates, you and each of those squadmates gain a +1 bonus to AC. If at least two of you are wielding shields, the bonus increases to +2. Hostile creatures treat the area within 5 feet of you as difficult terrain when moving toward you or your squadmates.
- **Veteran Commander (17th Level)**. You regain all expended uses of **Issue Orders** when you finish a short or long rest. When you use **Tactical Assessment**, you can grant its benefit to one squadmate until the end of your next turn.
- **The Line Holds (18th Level)**. Once per long rest when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, you can drop to 1 hit point instead. Until the end of your next turn, you and each squadmate within 15 feet of you have advantage on attack rolls and resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage. This feature is separate from **Relentless**; you can use both when reduced to 0 hit points if both are available, but you still end at 1 hit point.

### Arcaknight — Steel and Sigil

Arcaknights are Fighters who imbue might and magic by fighting against and alongside Inazria's spellcasters. Though they do not casts spells as a traditional Magi, Arcaknights **bind**, **break**, and **answer** magic with sigils, wards and anti-magic aura. Arcaknights are an elite force against eldritch evils.

**Philosophy**: Power through craft; the blade carries the spell.

**Sigilcraft**. Your arcane ability for Arcaknight features and techniques is **Intelligence**. Your **Arcaknight save DC** = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier.

**Sigils**. When you finish a long rest, you can inscribe up to two **sigil charges** on a weapon, shield, or suit of armor you touch. Some techniques expend a charge; expended charges are restored when you finish your next long rest (or one charge when you finish a short rest starting at 14th level). While you have at least one charged sigil on your equipment, weapon attacks with that equipment count as magical.

- **Rite of Binding (1st Level)**. You gain proficiency in the Arcana skill if you don't already have it.
- **Cantrip Etching (1st Level)**. Choose one minor working that requires no sigil charge: your inscribed weapon sheds bright light in a 10-foot radius and dim light for 10 feet more (on or off as a bonus action), or you can cast *detect magic* by touching an object (a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest, no components required).
- **Counter-Glyph (3rd Level)**. When you succeed on a saving throw against a spell or magical effect created by a creature you can see, you can use your reaction to deal force damage to that creature equal to your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier. You can use this reaction a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.
- **Arcane Ambience (5th Level)**. When a hostile creature you can see within 60 feet casts a spell, until the end of your next turn the first time each turn you hit that creature with a weapon attack, the attack deals extra force damage equal to your proficiency bonus.
- **Overcharge (9th Level)**. When you use an Arcaknight technique that would expend a sigil charge, you can spend 1 Exertion to forgo expending the charge or to double the technique's damage or ward duration (your choice). You can overcharge a given technique only once per turn.
- **Spellward Veteran (14th Level)**. When you finish a short rest, you can restore one expended sigil charge. When you use **Raise Ward**, the resistance and saving throw benefits last for 1 minute or until you use Raise Ward again (no concentration required).
- **Runebound Arsenal (18th Level)**. When you finish a long rest, you inscribe three sigil charges instead of two. Choose one weapon or shield to bear a **master rune**. The first time each turn you use **Sigil Strike** or **Raise Ward** with that item, you don't expend a sigil charge (all other limits still apply).

### Paladin — Oathbound Champion

Paladins are Fighters who answer to a Paragon. Where the Soldier serves a banner and the Arcaknight a sigil, the Paladin serves a sworn oath and the duty that flows from it. Each Paladin belongs to one of seven **orders**, each tied to one of the Paragons, but every order shares the same core discipline: hold the line, judge by your conscience, and let your conviction shape the battlefield.

In Inazria, Paladin loyalty is chosen by belief rather than birth. Any character may swear to any order, provided they keep its tenets.

**Philosophy**: Strength in service of sworn duty; the oath is the source.

**Devotion**. Paladin features draw on a resource called **Devotion**, separate from Exertion. Your Devotion pool equals your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier (minimum 1). You regain all expended Devotion when you finish a long rest, and you regain half your Devotion (rounded up) when you finish a short rest.

**Paladin save DC** = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier.

- **Lay on Hands (1st Level)**. You have a reservoir of healing power that replenishes when you finish a long rest. Your healing pool equals 5 × your fighter level. As an action, you can touch a willing creature (yourself included) and restore hit points to that creature, up to the maximum remaining in your pool. Alternatively, you can spend 5 points from your pool to end one disease or one instance of the poisoned condition affecting a creature you touch.
- **Oath Sense (1st Level)**. Your sworn vows attune you to the violations your order forbids (see Orders, below). Any creature within 30 feet of you that matches your order's tenets is considered **detected by your Oath Sense**, even if it is invisible or hidden by mundane means (but not behind total cover). You learn its presence and general direction. As an action, you can extend your awareness to 60 feet; until the end of your next turn, the detection range is 60 feet instead of 30 feet.
- **Oath Strike (3rd Level)**. You learn the **Oath Strike** Paladin technique at Familiar rank without spending technique points. You may still invest technique points to advance it to Practiced or Mastered. When you learn Oath Strike, choose one damage type from your order's three options (see Orders). This is your **Oath Damage** for all Oath Strike attacks, and you cannot change it without changing your order.
- **Sworn Boon (3rd Level)**. You gain the signature ability of your order (see Orders).
- **Aura of Conviction (5th Level)**. While you are conscious, you and friendly creatures within 10 feet of you have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened. Your order grants one additional aura effect (see Orders).
- **Cleansing Touch (6th Level)**. As an action, you can touch yourself or one willing creature within 5 feet and end one of the following on the target: the charmed, frightened, paralyzed, or poisoned condition, or one ongoing magical effect of 3rd level or lower (treat as *dispel magic* cast with a 3rd-level slot). You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Sacred Stride (7th Level)**. Your walking speed increases by 5 feet. You ignore difficult terrain while moving toward an ally within your Aura of Conviction. Magical effects that would reduce your speed have no effect on you.
- **Battle Prayer (9th Level)**. As a reaction when you or an ally within 30 feet that you can see fails an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can spend 1 Devotion to grant the failing creature advantage on a single reroll. The creature must use the new roll.
- **Aura's Reach (11th Level)**. The range of your Aura of Conviction increases to 15 feet.
- **Greater Aura (14th Level)**. While your Aura of Conviction is active, you and friendly creatures within its range gain resistance to one damage type chosen when you finish a long rest. Your order recommends a default damage type aligned with its work (see Orders), but you may choose any damage type.
- **Devout Resilience (17th Level)**. When you finish a short rest, you regain 1 expended Devotion in addition to the Devotion you normally regain on a short rest. The range of your Aura of Conviction increases to 30 feet.
- **Paragon's Mantle (18th Level)**. Once per long rest, as an action, you channel the authority of your Paragon directly. The effect lasts for 1 minute and is determined by your order (see Orders).

#### Orders

Each Paladin is sworn to one of seven orders. Your order shapes the violations you sense, the damage type you carry into the smite, the focus of your aura, the signature feature you gain at 3rd level, and the Mantle you channel at 18th. Everything else is shared across all Paladins.

##### The Concord

Paladins of the Concord serve Narthex's mandate of peace through arbitration. They are the visible knights of the treaty-roads, dispatched as neutral parties to feuds, border disputes, and broken truces. A Concord Paladin swears to offer parley before steel and to honor any truce they witness or declare.

- **Tenets**: Offer parley before steel. Honor a truce that you witness or declare. Never attack a creature that has accepted your parley unless they break it first.
- **Oath Sense detects**: creatures who have broken a sworn truce or treaty in the past day; creatures preparing to initiate violence against a non-combatant.
- **Oath Damage** (choose one at 3rd level): radiant, thunder, or psychic.
- **Aura addition**: Allies in your aura cannot be magically compelled to attack you or another ally in the aura.
- **Greater Aura recommended**: radiant.
- **Sworn Boon — Voice of the Concord (3rd Level)**. As an action, you demand a parley with one creature within 60 feet that can hear and understand you. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw against your Paladin save DC or be unable to take any hostile action against you or any willing ally within 5 feet of you for 1 minute. The effect ends early if you or any such ally takes a hostile action against the target or one of its allies, or if the target is harmed. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier, regaining all uses on a long rest.
- **Paragon's Mantle — Spear of Thilidan (18th Level)**. You declare a truce in a 60-foot radius centered on you. Choose up to six creatures in the area. For the duration, at the start of each of its turns, each chosen creature must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw against your Paladin save DC or be unable to take a hostile action against any other chosen creature on that turn. The first time a chosen creature successfully attacks another chosen creature during the duration, that attacker takes radiant damage equal to 8d8 + your Charisma modifier and is detected by your Oath Sense for the rest of the Mantle.

##### The Tempered

Paladins of the Tempered are Karhu's roving magistrates and executioners. They pursue international crime where no national power can reach. A Tempered Paladin tries, judges, and ends the unrepentant: the bandit-king, the slave-trader, the oathbreaker, the rogue magister.

- **Tenets**: Pursue the lawless across borders. Hear the accused before you strike. Show no mercy to the unrepentant.
- **Oath Sense detects**: creatures who have committed murder, kidnapping, slave-taking, or oathbreaking in the past month; creatures actively committing such a crime in your presence.
- **Oath Damage** (choose one at 3rd level): fire, necrotic, or psychic.
- **Aura addition**: Allies in your aura have advantage on saving throws to end being paralyzed, restrained, or stunned.
- **Greater Aura recommended**: fire.
- **Sworn Boon — Sentence (3rd Level)**. As a bonus action, you designate one creature you can see within 60 feet as **Convicted**. While Convicted, the creature is treated as detected by your Oath Sense, and you have advantage on Intimidation and Perception checks made against it. When you deal Oath Damage to a Convicted creature, the damage increases by your Charisma modifier. The Convicted condition lasts for 1 hour or until you designate a different creature as Convicted. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier, regaining all uses on a long rest.
- **Paragon's Mantle — Verdict (18th Level)**. Name up to three creatures within 60 feet as Convicted, overriding any prior Sentence. For the duration, each Convicted creature takes 4d8 damage of your Oath Damage type at the start of its turn and has disadvantage on saving throws against your Paladin features. If you reduce a Convicted creature to 0 hit points during the duration, you immediately regain Devotion equal to your Charisma modifier.

##### The Pillars of Kalindor

Paladins of the Pillars stand watch over the Great Ledger at Kalindor and over Valadorf himself. They also enforce the commercial law that binds the eight nations. A Pillar's shield is raised before their sword, and a Pillar's first oath is to defend.

- **Tenets**: Protect the Ledger, the Paragons, and the contracts that bind the nations. Honor every commercial agreement to which you bear witness. Hold the line that others depend on.
- **Oath Sense detects**: creatures who have broken a commercial agreement they personally signed within the past year; creatures bearing stolen goods of significant value.
- **Oath Damage** (choose one at 3rd level): force, thunder, or bludgeoning.
- **Aura addition**: Allies in your aura have advantage on saving throws to resist being moved against their will or knocked prone.
- **Greater Aura recommended**: bludgeoning.
- **Sworn Boon — Bulwark Stance (3rd Level)**. As a bonus action, you brace into Bulwark Stance. Until the start of your next turn, you gain a +2 bonus to AC and reduce all incoming damage by your proficiency bonus, but your speed is reduced by 10 feet. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier, regaining all uses on a long rest.
- **Paragon's Mantle — Living Pillar (18th Level)**. For the duration: you cannot be moved against your will; you cannot be knocked prone; and once during the Mantle, if damage would reduce you to 0 hit points, you instead drop to 1 hit point. Allies within your Aura of Conviction cannot be moved against their will or knocked prone. Any hostile creature that ends its turn within 30 feet of you takes force damage equal to your Charisma modifier (once per round per creature).

##### The Wardens of Will

The Wardens of Will train for decades in Ashiera's groves before they ever leave them. They hunt the threats other Paladins cannot see: the cult that has outlived its founder, the contract sealed by a coerced hand, the bloodline through which a hidden enemy works. A Warden moves quietly and acts only after long watching.

- **Tenets**: Watch first, act last. Reveal no more than is necessary in public. Trust the long pattern over the loud crisis.
- **Oath Sense detects**: creatures who are concealing their true intent toward you or those near you; creatures currently affected by an enchantment, possession, or other magical compulsion.
- **Oath Damage** (choose one at 3rd level): psychic, cold, or necrotic.
- **Aura addition**: Allies in your aura automatically perceive any creature within the aura that is invisible or hidden by mundane means.
- **Greater Aura recommended**: psychic.
- **Sworn Boon — Reading the Will (3rd Level)**. As a bonus action, you study one creature you can see within 60 feet. Choose one of the following: you learn whether the creature has hostile intent toward you or your allies; you learn whether the creature is lying about a single subject you can describe in one short phrase; or you learn whether the creature is currently affected by an enchantment, charm, or compulsion. The creature is unaware of this scrutiny. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier, regaining all uses on a long rest.
- **Paragon's Mantle — Cloak of Quietsong (18th Level)**. For the duration, you are invisible. This invisibility does not end if you attack or use a feature. You can take the Hide action as a bonus action. Whenever you hit a creature that has lied to you or any ally within the past hour, the attack deals psychic damage equal to 4d8 + your Charisma modifier in addition to its normal damage, and that creature becomes outlined in faint silver light to your allies for the rest of the Mantle.

##### The Shepherds

The Shepherds travel where civilization ends. They are sent to rescue those in peril far from any town — sailors lost to storm, caravans stranded in flood, pilgrims swallowed by the jungle — and to defend the wild places of Alore from those who would waste them.

- **Tenets**: Refuse no soul shelter from a storm. Defend the wild places. Travel light, and arrive in time.
- **Oath Sense detects**: creatures in mortal peril (at 0 HP, drowning, dying of exposure, falling, on fire, or otherwise about to die); creatures who have desecrated or polluted a natural site within the past month.
- **Oath Damage** (choose one at 3rd level): thunder, lightning, or cold.
- **Aura addition**: Allies in your aura have advantage on saving throws against exhaustion and against environmental damage (cold, heat, drowning, falling).
- **Greater Aura recommended**: thunder.
- **Sworn Boon — Storm's Sign (3rd Level)**. As a bonus action, you teleport up to 60 feet in a direct line to an unoccupied space within 5 feet of a creature you can see that is in mortal peril (as defined under Oath Sense detects). You can use this feature once per short or long rest. Additionally, you gain a swimming speed and a climbing speed each equal to your walking speed.
- **Paragon's Mantle — Calrien's Call (18th Level)**. You summon a localized storm in a 60-foot radius cylinder, 40 feet tall, centered on a point you can see within 60 feet. The storm lasts for the duration. While in the storm: hostile creatures treat the area as difficult terrain and have disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks involving sight or hearing; you and your allies suffer none of these penalties; you can teleport between unoccupied spaces within the storm as a bonus action; once per turn on your turn, you can hurl a thunderbolt at one creature in the storm as part of a weapon attack, dealing 4d8 thunder damage on a hit (in addition to the weapon's normal damage).

##### The Cleric

The Clerics travel the open road with Myrdin's same austere code: no heraldry, no payment beyond basic needs, no blade drawn first. They heal the sick, they teach the poor, and they bury the unnamed dead. They are also Inazria's most committed enemies of the undead.

- **Tenets**: Accept no payment beyond simple necessities (food, water and shelter). Heal the sick and teach the poor. Never draw your blade first. Destroy the undead wherever you find them.
- **Oath Sense detects**: undead creatures of any kind, automatically; creatures suffering from a grievous disease, infection, or untreated severe injury.
- **Oath Damage** (choose one at 3rd level): radiant, fire, or force.
- **Aura addition**: Allies in your aura have advantage on saving throws against disease and poison.
- **Greater Aura recommended**: necrotic.
- **Sworn Boon — Mercy's Hand (3rd Level)**. When you use Lay on Hands, you can also end one of the following on the target at no extra cost: the blinded condition, the deafened condition, or one ongoing disease. You also have advantage on weapon attack rolls against undead creatures.
- **Paragon's Mantle — Quiet Light (18th Level)**. You and each ally within 30 feet of you regain hit points equal to half of their hit point maximum. Each undead creature within 60 feet of you that can see you must make a Wisdom saving throw against your Paladin save DC. On a failed save, an undead of CR 5 or lower is destroyed, and an undead of higher CR takes 8d8 radiant damage and is frightened of you for the rest of the Mantle. On a successful save, the undead takes 4d8 radiant damage.

##### The Arbiters

The Arbiters serve Ostro's mandate of true counsel. They advise kings, magistrates, and adventuring companies; they investigate magical mysteries; they keep the truth on the record. An Arbiter's word is binding because it is never spoken without weight.

- **Tenets**: Never withhold a true word in counsel. Never speak a false word in counsel. Know before you act.
- **Oath Sense detects**: creatures who have spoken a material lie that affected another in the past day; creatures carrying or concealing magical writing they are forbidden from bearing.
- **Oath Damage** (choose one at 3rd level): force, psychic, or lightning.
- **Aura addition**: Allies in your aura have advantage on Intelligence checks to recall lore (history, religion, arcana) and to identify magical effects.
- **Greater Aura recommended**: psychic.
- **Sworn Boon — Rites of the Library (3rd Level)**. You gain proficiency in one of: Arcana, History, Investigation, or Religion (choose at 3rd level). You also choose two **rites** from the following list: *detect magic*, *comprehend languages*, *identify*, *sending*, *speak with dead*. As an action, you can cast each chosen rite without using a spell slot or material components. You can cast each chosen rite once per long rest. At 11th level, choose one additional rite from the list, and add the following to the available choices: *augury*, *clairvoyance*, *speak with animals*, *speak with plants*, *tongues*, *zone of truth*.
- **Paragon's Mantle — Verdant Truth (18th Level)**. You speak a single Word of true reckoning. For the duration, any creature within 60 feet of you that can hear you and understand at least one language must answer any question you put to them honestly and completely, or take 3d8 psychic damage on each refusal. A creature can attempt a Wisdom saving throw against your Paladin save DC at the start of each of its turns; on a success, that creature is unaffected by Verdant Truth for the rest of the Mantle. Creatures unaffected by Verdant Truth do not have to answer your questions and do not take damage for refusing.

### Dragoon — Sky-Spear Cavalry

Elite lancers and skirmishers trained for mounted charges, leaping assaults, and hit-and-run warfare. Dragoons trade heavy static defense for mobility, reach, and devastating committed attacks from above or at full gallop.

**Philosophy**: Strike from where the enemy cannot answer.

**Planned techniques**: Lunging Strike, Aerial Vault, Impaling Charge, Tactical Retreat.
