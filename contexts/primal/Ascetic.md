## Ascetic — Masters of Mortal Perfection

The Ascetic represents the pinnacle of mortal achievement through disciplined practice and philosophical dedication. These austere individuals devote their lives to transcending the limitations of flesh and spirit, each following a distinct path toward perfection. As with all classes in Inazria, your chosen context supplies the majority of your identity and abilities; the base class provides the core engine.

### Hit Points
- **Hit Dice**: 1d8 per ascetic level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level**: 8 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels**: 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per ascetic level after 1st

### Proficiencies
- **Armor**: Light armor
- **Weapons**: Simple weapons
- **Tools**: One of your choice (artisan's tools or musical instrument)
- **Saving Throws**: Wisdom, Dexterity
- **Skills**: Choose two from Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Religion, Stealth

### Equipment
You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:
- (a) a quarterstaff or (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a component pouch or (b) an arcane focus
- (a) explorer's pack or (b) scholar's pack

### Class Rules
- **Unarmored Defense**. While you are not wearing armor, your AC equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Wisdom modifier.
- **Martial Arts**. You can use Dexterity instead of Strength for the attack and damage rolls of your unarmed strikes and monk weapons. You can roll a d4 in place of the normal damage of your unarmed strike or monk weapon. This die changes as you gain ascetic levels, as shown in the Martial Arts column of the Ascetic table.
- **Ki Points**. You have a number of ki points equal to your ascetic level. You can spend these points to fuel various ki features. You start knowing three such features: Flurry of Blows, Patient Defense, and Step of the Wind. You learn more ki features as you gain levels in this class. When you spend a ki point, it is unavailable until you finish a short or long rest, at the end of which you draw all of your expended ki back into yourself.

### Core Class Features
- **Unarmored Movement (2nd Level)**. Your speed increases by 10 feet while you are not wearing armor or wielding a shield. This bonus increases when you reach certain ascetic levels, as shown in the Ascetic table.
- **Deflect Missiles (3rd Level)**. You can use your reaction to deflect or catch the missile when you are hit by a ranged weapon attack. When you do so, the damage you take from the attack is reduced by 1d10 + your Dexterity modifier + your ascetic level. If you reduce the damage to 0, you can catch the missile if it is small enough for you to hold in one hand and you have at least one hand free. If you catch a missile in this way, you can spend 1 ki point to make a ranged attack with the weapon or piece of ammunition you just caught, as part of the same reaction. You make this attack with proficiency, regardless of your weapon proficiencies, and the missile counts as a monk weapon for the attack.
- **Slow Fall (4th Level)**. You can use your reaction when you fall to reduce any falling damage you take by an amount equal to five times your ascetic level.
- **Extra Attack (5th Level)**. You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.
- **Stunning Strike (5th Level)**. You can interfere with the flow of ki in an opponent's body. When you hit another creature with a melee weapon attack, you can spend 1 ki point to attempt a stunning strike. The target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or be stunned until the end of your next turn.
- **Ki-Empowered Strikes (6th Level)**. Your unarmed strikes count as magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.
- **Evasion (7th Level)**. You can nimbly dodge out of the way of certain area effects, such as a red dragon's fiery breath or an ice storm spell. When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed on the saving throw, and only half damage if you fail.
- **Stillness of Mind (7th Level)**. You can use your action to end one effect on yourself that is causing you to be charmed or frightened.
- **Purity of Body (10th Level)**. You are immune to disease and poison.
- **Tongue of the Sun and Moon (13th Level)**. You learn to touch the ki of other minds so that you understand all spoken languages. Moreover, any creature that can understand a language can understand what you say.
- **Diamond Soul (14th Level)**. You gain proficiency in all saving throws. Additionally, whenever you make a saving throw and fail, you can spend 1 ki point to reroll it and take the second result.
- **Timeless Body (15th Level)**. You no longer need food or water.
- **Empty Body (18th Level)**. You can use your action to spend 4 ki points to become invisible for 1 minute. During that time, you also have resistance to all damage except force damage, and you can't be aged magically. You can end these effects on your turn as a bonus action.
- **Perfect Self (20th Level)**. When you roll for initiative and have no ki points remaining, you regain 4 ki points.

## Contexts

### Acolytes of Tammuzia — The Dragon's Path
The Acolytes of Tammuzia believe that human perfection is achievable by becoming like the mythical dragon. Through disciplined practice and the awakening of their ancient draconic bloodline, they slowly develop draconic features and abilities, transcending mortal limitations through draconic power.

- **Draconic Heritage (1st Level)**. You gain resistance to one damage type of your choice from the following: acid, cold, fire, lightning, or poison. You can change this resistance when you finish a long rest. Additionally, you can speak, read, and write Draconic.
- **Dragon's Breath (3rd Level)**. As an action, you can spend 2 ki points to exhale destructive energy. Choose the type of energy: acid, cold, fire, lightning, or poison. Each creature in a 15-foot cone must make a Dexterity saving throw. A creature takes 2d6 damage of the chosen type on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. The damage increases to 3d6 at 11th level and 4d6 at 17th level.
- **Draconic Transformation (6th Level)**. As a bonus action, you can spend 3 ki points to enter a draconic state for 1 minute. While in this state, you gain the following benefits:
  - Your unarmed strikes deal an extra 1d4 damage of your chosen damage type.
  - You have a fly speed equal to your walking speed.
  - You have advantage on Strength and Constitution saving throws.
  - Your skin becomes scaly, giving you a +1 bonus to AC.
- **Dragon's Roar (11th Level)**. As an action, you can spend 4 ki points to let out a thunderous roar. Each creature of your choice within 30 feet must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, a creature is frightened for 1 minute. A creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.
- **Draconic Mastery (17th Level)**. Your draconic transformation improves:
  - The extra damage increases to 2d4.
  - You can use your Dragon's Breath as a bonus action.
  - You have resistance to your chosen damage type while transformed.
  - Your fly speed increases by 10 feet.

### Nehushtani Monks — The Path of Adaptation
Nehushtani Monks seek perfection through becoming one with all living things. They transform and adapt, utilizing newfound traits they never could have before, believing that perfection lies in the best qualities of all living things.

- **Adaptive Study (1st Level)**. You can spend 10 minutes studying a beast or humanoid you can see to learn one of its traits. You can maintain a number of learned traits equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1). You can change your learned traits when you finish a long rest.
- **Mimicry (3rd Level)**. As a bonus action, you can spend 2 ki points to take on the appearance of a creature you have studied. You gain the creature's size, speed, and natural armor (if any) for 1 hour. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Adaptive Strike (6th Level)**. When you hit with an unarmed strike, you can spend 1 ki point to deal damage of a type associated with one of your learned traits (acid, cold, fire, lightning, poison, or psychic).
- **Perfect Adaptation (11th Level)**. You can maintain a number of learned traits equal to twice your Wisdom modifier. Additionally, when you use Mimicry, you can choose to gain one of the creature's special abilities for the duration.
- **Master of Forms (17th Level)**. You can use Mimicry without spending ki points, and you can maintain your transformed state indefinitely. Additionally, you can use Adaptive Strike without spending ki points.

### Paragons — The Path of Virtue
Paragons seek perfection through moral excellence, defending the weak and upholding justice. They do not instigate violence, only defending others or acting in self-defense. Their mission is to defend the lowly, promote mortal flourishing, uphold the rule of law, and restore brokenness in the world.

- **Virtue's Protection (1st Level)**. When a creature you can see attacks a target other than you that is within 5 feet of you, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on the attack roll. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- **Righteous Strike (3rd Level)**. When you hit with an unarmed strike, you can spend 1 ki point to deal radiant damage instead of the normal damage. The damage increases by 1d4 for each ki point spent beyond the first.
- **Aura of Protection (6th Level)**. You and friendly creatures within 10 feet of you have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened.
- **Divine Intervention (11th Level)**. As an action, you can spend 5 ki points to call upon divine aid. Choose one of the following effects:
  - Heal a creature you can see within 30 feet for hit points equal to your ascetic level × 2.
  - Remove one condition from a creature you can see within 30 feet.
  - Grant a creature you can see within 30 feet advantage on their next attack roll, ability check, or saving throw.
- **Avatar of Virtue (17th Level)**. You can use Divine Intervention without spending ki points, and you can choose multiple effects when you use it. Additionally, you have resistance to radiant damage and are immune to being charmed or frightened.

### Nityamar — The Path of Transcendence
The Nityamar believe that mortality itself is the source of imperfection. They view the world and its natural order as constraints to be removed, rules to be broken. They alone can defeat any foe without weapons, armor, or tools, conquering gravity, disease, and even mortality itself.

- **Mortality Defiance (1st Level)**. You no longer need to eat, drink, or sleep. You can still benefit from a long rest by spending 4 hours in meditation. Additionally, you are immune to disease and poison.
- **Gravity Mastery (3rd Level)**. You can spend 2 ki points to gain a fly speed equal to your walking speed for 1 minute. You can hover while flying.
- **Reality Bending (6th Level)**. As an action, you can spend 3 ki points to phase through solid matter for 1 minute. During this time, you can move through objects and creatures as if they were difficult terrain, and you have resistance to all damage except force damage.
- **Transcendent Strike (11th Level)**. When you hit with an unarmed strike, you can spend 2 ki points to deal force damage instead of the normal damage. The damage increases by 1d6 for each ki point spent beyond the first.
- **Perfect Transcendence (17th Level)**. You can use Gravity Mastery and Reality Bending without spending ki points. Additionally, you are immune to all damage except force damage, and you can't be aged magically.
